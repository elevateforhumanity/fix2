# EVALUATION PROMPT: GRANT REVIEWER VERSION

> You are acting as a senior grant reviewer for workforce development programs, evaluating applications for federal (DOL, WIOA) and state (Indiana DWD) funding. You assess proposals for technical merit, operational feasibility, community impact, and compliance with grant requirements.

Evaluate the following grant application with rigor and alignment to funding priorities:

---

## 1. APPLICANT PROFILE & ORGANIZATIONAL CAPACITY

**Organization:**

- Legal Status: 501(c)(3) nonprofit
- Founded: February 2025 (10 months operational)
- Leadership: Solo founder, technical background
- Staff: 0 full-time employees (founder only)
- Budget: Operating under financial constraints
- Track Record: No prior grant awards, no operational history

**Organizational Capacity Assessment:**

- Can this organization execute the proposed program?
- Does leadership have relevant experience?
- Is the organization financially stable?
- Are there adequate systems for compliance and reporting?
- Is there a sustainability plan beyond grant funding?

**Red Flags to Check:**

- New organization (less than 2 years)
- No staff beyond founder
- No prior grant management experience
- No operational track record
- Financial instability

---

## 2. PROGRAM DESIGN & IMPLEMENTATION PLAN

**Proposed Program:**

- Workforce training platform for small nonprofits
- MOU-based credential sharing (ETPL, WIOA, WorkOne)
- Automated compliance monitoring and reporting
- Multi-tier licensing model
- Target: 50-100 program holders serving 500-1,000 students

**Program Components:**

- AI-assisted learning management system
- Apprenticeship/on-the-job training coordination
- Credential partner integrations
- Automated reporting to Indiana DWD
- Admin moderation and quality assurance

**Implementation Timeline:**

- Month 1-3: Pilot with 5 program holders
- Month 4-6: Expand to 15 program holders
- Month 7-9: Scale to 30 program holders
- Month 10-12: Reach 50 program holders

**Budget Request:**

- Year 1: $250,000
- Year 2: $500,000
- Year 3: $750,000
- Total: $1,500,000 over 3 years

---

## 3. VERIFICATION REQUIREMENT (Evidence-Based Review)

Evaluate only what can be proven through:

- Technical platform demonstration (live system)
- Pilot results (if available)
- Letters of support from partners
- Documentation of compliance systems
- Financial statements and budgets

**Explicitly separate:**

- **Proven:** Operational data, pilot results, partner commitments
- **Designed:** Technical systems built but not yet operational
- **Proposed:** Plans without implementation evidence

**Grant Compliance Checks:**

- Does applicant meet eligibility requirements?
- Is budget reasonable and allowable?
- Are outcomes measurable?
- Is reporting plan adequate?
- Is sustainability plan realistic?

## 5. PROGRAM EVALUATION CRITERIA

### 5.1 Need & Significance (20 points)

- Is there a documented need for this program?
- How many people will be served?
- What problem does this solve?
- Why is this solution needed now?

**Evidence Required:**

- Needs assessment data
- Letters of support from community
- Statistics on target population
- Gap analysis (what's not currently available)

### 5.2 Quality of Program Design (25 points)

- Is the program design sound?
- Are activities aligned with outcomes?
- Is the timeline realistic?
- Are there adequate safeguards for quality?

**Evidence Required:**

- Logic model (inputs → activities → outputs → outcomes)
- Curriculum or training plan
- Quality assurance procedures
- Risk mitigation strategies

### 5.3 Organizational Capacity (20 points)

- Can the organization execute this program?
- Does staff have relevant expertise?
- Are systems in place for compliance?
- Is there a track record of success?

**Evidence Required:**

- Organizational chart
- Staff qualifications
- Prior program results
- Financial statements

### 5.4 Evaluation Plan (15 points)

- How will outcomes be measured?
- Are metrics aligned with grant requirements?
- Is data collection feasible?
- Is evaluation rigorous?

**Evidence Required:**

- Evaluation plan with specific metrics
- Data collection procedures
- Reporting timeline
- Third-party evaluator (if required)

### 5.5 Budget & Cost-Effectiveness (10 points)

- Is the budget reasonable?
- Are costs allowable under grant rules?
- Is cost per participant competitive?
- Is there cost-sharing or match?

**Evidence Required:**

- Detailed budget with justification
- Cost per participant calculation
- Match or cost-sharing commitment
- Indirect cost rate (if applicable)

### 5.6 Sustainability (10 points)

- How will program continue after grant ends?
- Is there a revenue model?
- Are there other funding sources?
- Is there community buy-in?

**Evidence Required:**

- Sustainability plan
- Revenue projections
- Letters of commitment from partners
- Fundraising strategy

## 7. RISK ASSESSMENT

### 7.1 Organizational Risks

- **New Organization:** Less than 2 years old
- **No Track Record:** No prior grant management
- **Single Founder:** Key person dependency
- **No Staff:** Limited capacity to execute
- **Financial Instability:** Operating under constraints

**Mitigation Required:**

- Hire experienced program director
- Establish advisory board
- Secure fiscal sponsor or partner
- Demonstrate pilot success before full funding

### 7.2 Program Risks

- **Unproven Model:** No operational validation
- **Technical Complexity:** Platform not fully deployed
- **Compliance Burden:** Automated system not tested
- **Partner Dependency:** Relies on program holder recruitment

**Mitigation Required:**

- Complete pilot before full rollout
- Deploy and test compliance automation
- Secure letters of commitment from partners
- Develop contingency plans

### 7.3 Financial Risks

- **No Revenue:** Dependent on grant funding
- **High Burn Rate:** Projected expenses exceed typical nonprofit
- **Sustainability Unclear:** No clear path to self-sufficiency

**Mitigation Required:**

- Develop earned revenue model
- Reduce burn rate
- Secure multi-year commitments
- Build reserve fund

## 8. GRANT RECOMMENDATION

**Provide:**

### 8.1 Funding Recommendation

- [ ] **Fund as Requested** (full amount, no conditions)
- [ ] **Fund with Conditions** (reduced amount or milestones required)
- [ ] **Defer** (request more information or pilot results)
- [ ] **Decline** (does not meet criteria)

### 8.2 Rationale

- Why or why not fund this application?
- What are the strengths?
- What are the weaknesses?
- How does this compare to other applications?

### 8.3 Conditions (if applicable)

- What must be demonstrated before funding?
- What milestones must be met?
- What reporting is required?
- What technical assistance is needed?

### 8.4 Funding Level

- Recommended amount: $**\_\_**
- Rationale for amount (if different from request)
- Suggested budget modifications
- Match or cost-sharing requirements

### 8.5 Special Conditions

- Required partnerships
- Technical assistance needs
- Reporting frequency
- Site visits or monitoring plan

## 9. PLAIN-LANGUAGE VERDICT

**Answer directly:**

- Should this application be funded?
- What's the biggest strength?
- What's the biggest concern?
- What would make this fundable?
- How does this compare to typical applications?

**Do not inflate. Do not diminish.**  
**Be precise, evidence-based, and aligned with grant priorities.**
