## VIDEO 1: Program Holder Compliance Training (25 minutes)

### Script: "Complete Guide to Program Compliance & Documentation"

```
[INTRO - 0:00-1:00]

Welcome to the Program Holder Compliance Training. I'm going to walk you through everything you need to know about compliance requirements, documentation, and getting paid for your training services.

This is critical information. Following these guidelines ensures you get paid on time, students get the support they need, and we all stay compliant with WIOA, WRG, and other funding requirements.

Let's get started.

[SECTION 1: PAYMENT STRUCTURE - 1:00-5:00]

First, let's talk about how you get paid. Your revenue share is 1/3 of the grant funding per student, but it's paid in three installments based on student progress.

Here's the breakdown:

25% AT STUDENT START
You receive 25% of your share when the student enrolls and attends their first day of class.

For example, if a student is funded at $5,000, your total share is $1,667. 
At start, you receive 25% of that, which is $417.

35% AT MIDPOINT
When the student reaches 50% program completion, you receive 35% of your share.
That's $583 in our example.

40% AT COMPLETION
When the student successfully completes the program with all required documentation, you receive the final 40%.
That's $667 in our example.

Total: $417 + $583 + $667 = $1,667 per student.

This performance-based payment structure ensures students are progressing and completing programs, which is what funders require.

[SECTION 2: REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION - 5:00-12:00]

Now, let's talk about the documentation required for each payment. This is non-negotiable. Without proper documentation, payments will be delayed or denied.

AT START - TO RECEIVE YOUR 25% PAYMENT:

1. ENROLLMENT APPLICATION
   - Completed and signed by student
   - All fields filled out accurately
   - Emergency contact information included

2. WIOA ELIGIBILITY DETERMINATION
   - Provided by Elevate or workforce board
   - Must show student qualifies for funding
   - Keep a copy in student file

3. ATTENDANCE VERIFICATION
   - Student attended first week of classes
   - Sign-in sheets or attendance log
   - Minimum 80% attendance in first week

4. SIGNED TRAINING AGREEMENT
   - Student acknowledges program requirements
   - Attendance policy understood
   - Code of conduct signed

5. ORIENTATION COMPLETION
   - Student completed online orientation
   - Competency test passed (70% or higher)
   - Certificate of completion

Submit these documents within 5 business days of student start to receive your 25% payment.

AT MIDPOINT - TO RECEIVE YOUR 35% PAYMENT:

1. ATTENDANCE LOG
   - Student has completed 50% of program hours
   - Minimum 80% attendance maintained
   - Any absences documented with reasons

2. PROGRESS REPORT
   - Current grades in all subjects
   - Skills assessment results
   - Areas of strength and improvement

3. INSTRUCTOR EVALUATION
   - Professional conduct assessment
   - Technical skills progress
   - Attendance and punctuality

4. MID-PROGRAM COUNSELING NOTES
   - One-on-one meeting with student
   - Discussion of progress and goals
   - Any support needs identified

5. CONTINUED ELIGIBILITY VERIFICATION
   - Confirm student still qualifies for funding
   - No changes in circumstances
   - Updated contact information

Submit these documents when student reaches 50% completion to receive your 35% payment.

AT COMPLETION - TO RECEIVE YOUR 40% PAYMENT:

1. CERTIFICATE OF COMPLETION
   - Official certificate with your signature
   - Date of completion
   - Program name and hours

2. FINAL TRANSCRIPT
   - Final grades in all subjects
   - Overall GPA or pass/fail status
   - Skills assessments completed

3. ATTENDANCE RECORD
   - Complete attendance log for entire program
   - Must show 80% or higher attendance
   - Excused vs. unexcused absences documented

4. INDUSTRY CERTIFICATION
   - If applicable to your program
   - Copy of certification or exam results
   - Certification number and expiration date

5. EXIT INTERVIEW DOCUMENTATION
   - Final meeting with student
   - Career goals and job search plan
   - Feedback on program

6. JOB PLACEMENT VERIFICATION
   - If student secured employment
   - Employer name and contact
   - Start date and wage information

7. PIRL DATA SUBMISSION
   - All required WIOA data points
   - Submitted through Elevate portal
   - Verified for accuracy

8. FINAL COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST
   - All required documents collected
   - Student file complete
   - Ready for audit

Submit these documents within 10 business days of student completion to receive your final 40% payment.

[SECTION 3: COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS - 12:00-18:00]

Beyond documentation, there are ongoing compliance requirements you must follow:

ATTENDANCE TRACKING
- Take attendance every class session
- Document absences and reasons
- Contact students after 2 consecutive absences
- Report excessive absences to Elevate immediately

Students must maintain 80% attendance to remain eligible for funding. If a student drops below 80%, they may be terminated from the program and you will not receive remaining payments for that student.

PROGRESS MONITORING
- Assess student progress weekly
- Provide feedback and support
- Document interventions for struggling students
- Report concerns to Elevate early

PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT
- Maintain appropriate instructor-student boundaries
- Follow code of conduct
- Report any incidents immediately
- Protect student privacy (FERPA compliance)

FACILITY & SAFETY
- Maintain safe learning environment
- Follow OSHA guidelines if applicable
- Keep equipment in good working order
- Have emergency procedures posted

RECORD KEEPING
- Maintain student files for 7 years
- Keep all documentation organized
- Respond to audit requests within 48 hours
- Protect confidential information

[SECTION 4: COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID - 18:00-22:00]

Let me share the most common mistakes that delay payments:

MISTAKE #1: INCOMPLETE DOCUMENTATION
Missing signatures, blank fields, or partial forms will delay your payment. Double-check everything before submitting.

MISTAKE #2: LATE SUBMISSIONS
Submit documents on time. Late submissions mean late payments. Set reminders for yourself.

MISTAKE #3: POOR ATTENDANCE TRACKING
Inconsistent or inaccurate attendance records are a red flag in audits. Take attendance every single class.

MISTAKE #4: NOT REPORTING ISSUES EARLY
If a student is struggling, absent, or having problems, tell us immediately. Don't wait until it's too late.

MISTAKE #5: MISSING DEADLINES
Know your program deadlines and stick to them. Late completions may not be reimbursed.

[SECTION 5: GETTING HELP - 22:00-24:00]

You're not alone in this. We're here to support you.

COMPLIANCE QUESTIONS
Email: compliance@elevateforhumanity.org
Phone: (317) 314-3757
Response time: Within 24 hours

DOCUMENT SUBMISSION
Portal: elevateforhumanity.org/program-holder
Upload documents directly to student files
You'll receive confirmation within 48 hours

PAYMENT QUESTIONS
Email: payments@elevateforhumanity.org
Payment schedule: 15th and 30th of each month
Direct deposit available

TECHNICAL SUPPORT
Email: support@elevateforhumanity.org
Portal issues, login problems, technical questions
Available Monday-Friday, 8am-6pm

[CLOSING - 24:00-25:00]

Remember: Compliance isn't just paperwork. It's about ensuring students succeed, funders are satisfied, and you get paid fairly for your work.

Follow these guidelines, submit documentation on time, and communicate with us regularly. We're partners in this mission to transform lives through education.

Thank you for being part of the Elevate for Humanity network. Now let's go change some lives!

[END]

## VIDEO 2: Student Course Completion Requirements (20 minutes)

### Script: "Your Path to Completion & Career Success"

```
[INTRO - 0:00-1:00]

Congratulations on enrolling in your training program! You're taking an important step toward a new career, and we're here to support you every step of the way.

In this video, I'm going to explain exactly what you need to do to successfully complete your program, what support is available to you, and how to qualify for additional benefits like OJT pay and JRI stipends.

Let's make sure you have everything you need to succeed.

[SECTION 1: PROGRAM COMPLETION REQUIREMENTS - 1:00-5:00]

To successfully complete your program and receive your certificate, you must meet these requirements:

ATTENDANCE: 80% OR HIGHER
This is the most important requirement. You must attend at least 80% of all scheduled classes.

For example, if your program has 100 class hours, you can miss no more than 20 hours.

Why is this so important? Because funding requires it. If you drop below 80% attendance, you may be terminated from the program.

If you need to miss class:
- Contact your instructor BEFORE class
- Provide a valid reason
- Make up any missed work
- Get back on track immediately

GRADES: PASSING IN ALL SUBJECTS
You must maintain passing grades in all subjects. What "passing" means depends on your program:
- Some programs require 70% or higher
- Some programs are pass/fail
- Your instructor will explain your program's requirements

SKILLS ASSESSMENTS: DEMONSTRATE COMPETENCY
You'll be assessed on practical skills throughout the program. You must demonstrate competency in all required skills before completion.

PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT: FOLLOW CODE OF CONDUCT
- Arrive on time
- Treat instructors and classmates with respect
- Follow safety rules
- No drugs, alcohol, or weapons
- Maintain professional behavior

FINAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Complete all assignments
- Pass final exam (if applicable)
- Complete exit interview
- Submit job search documentation
- Provide feedback on program

[SECTION 2: SUPPORT SERVICES AVAILABLE - 5:00-8:00]

You're not in this alone. We provide support services to help you succeed:

CASE MANAGEMENT
Your case manager will:
- Check in with you regularly
- Help solve problems
- Connect you with resources
- Support your success

CAREER COUNSELING
- Resume writing assistance
- Interview preparation
- Job search strategies
- Career planning

SUPPORTIVE SERVICES
If you need help with:
- Transportation
- Childcare
- Work clothing/uniforms
- Tools or supplies
- Emergency assistance

Talk to your case manager. We may be able to help.

TUTORING & ACADEMIC SUPPORT
If you're struggling with coursework:
- Ask your instructor for help
- Request a tutor
- Attend study groups
- Use online resources

Don't wait until you're failing. Get help early!

[SECTION 3: OJT PAY - ON-THE-JOB TRAINING - 8:00-13:00]

Now let's talk about OJT - On-the-Job Training. This is a program where you can get PAID while you complete training with an employer.

WHAT IS OJT?

OJT is a work-based learning program where an employer hires you and trains you on the job. The employer receives a wage reimbursement to offset the cost of training you.

You get:
- A real job with a real paycheck
- Hands-on training
- Work experience
- Potential for permanent employment

The employer gets:
- Reimbursement for 50% of your wages during training
- A trained employee
- Support from Elevate

HOW MUCH DO YOU GET PAID?

You earn the employer's regular wage for that position. This is NOT a stipend - it's a real job with real wages.

For example:
- HVAC trainee: $15-$18/hour
- Medical assistant trainee: $14-$16/hour
- Welding trainee: $16-$20/hour

You'll work 20-40 hours per week depending on the position and your availability.

WHO QUALIFIES FOR OJT?

To qualify for OJT, you must:

1. BE ENROLLED IN A TRAINING PROGRAM
   - Currently attending classes
   - Maintaining 80% attendance
   - Passing grades

2. MEET WIOA ELIGIBILITY
   - Low income, or
   - Dislocated worker, or
   - Youth (16-24), or
   - Other WIOA categories

3. HAVE COMPLETED ORIENTATION
   - Passed competency test
   - Understand work expectations
   - Ready for employment

4. PASS EMPLOYER SCREENING
   - Interview with employer
   - Background check (if required)
   - Drug test (if required)
   - Meet employer requirements

5. COMMIT TO THE TRAINING PERIOD
   - Usually 3-6 months
   - Full-time or part-time
   - Complete training plan

HOW TO APPLY FOR OJT:

1. Talk to your case manager
2. Express interest in OJT
3. Complete OJT application
4. Interview with employers
5. Get matched with a position
6. Start working and earning!

IMPORTANT: OJT is competitive. Not everyone will be selected. Priority goes to students with:
- Excellent attendance
- Strong performance
- Professional conduct
- Employer recommendations

[SECTION 4: JRI STIPENDS - JUSTICE REINVESTMENT INITIATIVE - 13:00-18:00]

JRI is a special program for individuals with criminal justice involvement. If you qualify, you can receive a STIPEND while you complete training.

WHAT IS JRI?

The Justice Reinvestment Initiative provides stipends to help justice-involved individuals complete training and secure employment. This is grant funding specifically for re-entry participants.

HOW MUCH IS THE STIPEND?

JRI stipends are typically:
- $200-$400 per week
- Paid bi-weekly
- For the duration of your training program
- Up to 6 months maximum

The exact amount depends on:
- Your program length
- Available funding
- Your attendance and progress

WHO QUALIFIES FOR JRI STIPENDS?

To qualify for JRI, you must meet ALL of these requirements:

1. CRIMINAL JUSTICE INVOLVEMENT
   - Currently on probation or parole, OR
   - Released from incarceration within past 5 years, OR
   - Diverted from criminal justice system

2. INDIANA RESIDENT
   - Live in Indiana
   - Provide proof of residency
   - Marion County residents prioritized

3. ENROLLED IN APPROVED TRAINING
   - Healthcare, trades, or other high-demand field
   - Minimum 20 hours per week
   - Leading to industry certification

4. MEET INCOME REQUIREMENTS
   - Low income (under 200% poverty level)
   - Unemployed or underemployed
   - Financial need documented

5. CASE MANAGEMENT PARTICIPATION
   - Meet with case manager weekly
   - Follow Individual Service Plan
   - Participate in support services

6. MAINTAIN PROGRAM REQUIREMENTS
   - 80% attendance minimum
   - Passing grades
   - Professional conduct
   - Drug-free (random testing may apply)

HOW TO APPLY FOR JRI STIPENDS:

1. DISCLOSE YOUR BACKGROUND
   - Be honest about criminal history
   - Provide documentation
   - Explain your situation

2. COMPLETE JRI APPLICATION
   - Detailed application form
   - Income verification
   - Proof of justice involvement
   - References

3. MEET WITH CASE MANAGER
   - Develop Individual Service Plan
   - Set goals and milestones
   - Identify support needs

4. GET APPROVED
   - Application reviewed
   - Funding availability confirmed
   - Stipend amount determined

5. MAINTAIN ELIGIBILITY
   - Attend all classes
   - Meet with case manager
   - Follow program rules
   - Submit required documentation

IMPORTANT JRI RULES:

- Stipends are for TRAINING ONLY. You cannot work full-time and receive stipends.
- You must report ANY income to your case manager immediately.
- Missing appointments or classes may result in stipend suspension.
- Positive drug tests may result in termination from JRI.
- Criminal activity will result in immediate termination.

[SECTION 5: COMBINING BENEFITS - 18:00-19:00]

CAN YOU RECEIVE BOTH OJT PAY AND JRI STIPENDS?

No. You cannot receive both at the same time. Here's why:

- OJT is employment. You're earning wages.
- JRI stipends are for training when you're NOT employed.

You must choose one or the other.

WHICH SHOULD YOU CHOOSE?

OJT is usually better because:
- Higher pay (real wages vs. stipend)
- Work experience
- Potential for permanent job
- Builds your resume

But JRI may be better if:
- You need flexibility for other obligations
- You're not ready for full employment
- You need more training first
- OJT positions aren't available

Talk to your case manager about what's best for your situation.

[CLOSING - 19:00-20:00]

You have everything you need to succeed:
- Clear requirements for completion
- Support services to help you
- Opportunities for OJT pay or JRI stipends
- A team that believes in you

Your job is to:
- Show up every day
- Do your best work
- Ask for help when you need it
- Stay focused on your goals

We're here to support you every step of the way. You've got this!

Welcome to Elevate for Humanity. Let's build your future together.

[END]
```

## VIDEO 3: OJT & JRI Quick Reference Guide (10 minutes)

### Script: "OJT vs JRI - Which One Is Right for You?"

```
[INTRO - 0:00-1:00]

Quick question: Do you want to get PAID while you train?

If yes, you have two options: OJT or JRI.

Let me break down the differences so you can choose the right path for you.

[SECTION 1: OJT OVERVIEW - 1:00-4:00]

OJT = ON-THE-JOB TRAINING

What it is:
- A real job with a real employer
- You get paid regular wages
- Employer trains you on the job
- Employer gets reimbursed for training costs

Who qualifies:
- WIOA eligible
- Enrolled in training program
- Good attendance and grades
- Pass employer screening

How much you earn:
- $14-$20/hour (depends on job)
- 20-40 hours per week
- Real paycheck, not a stipend

How long:
- 3-6 months typically
- Can lead to permanent employment

Best for:
- People ready to work
- Those who need income NOW
- Building work experience
- Getting hired permanently

[SECTION 2: JRI OVERVIEW - 4:00-7:00]

JRI = JUSTICE REINVESTMENT INITIATIVE

What it is:
- A stipend while you complete training
- For people with criminal justice involvement
- Helps you focus on training without working

Who qualifies:
- Criminal justice involvement (probation, parole, or recent release)
- Indiana resident
- Low income
- Enrolled in approved training
- Meet with case manager weekly

How much you get:
- $200-$400 per week
- Paid bi-weekly
- For duration of training (up to 6 months)

Requirements:
- 80% attendance
- Passing grades
- Weekly case management
- Drug-free
- Cannot work full-time

Best for:
- People with criminal background
- Those who need flexibility
- Focusing on training first
- Not ready for full employment yet

[SECTION 3: SIDE-BY-SIDE COMPARISON - 7:00-9:00]

Let's compare them directly:

MONEY:
- OJT: $14-$20/hour = $560-$800/week (40 hours)
- JRI: $200-$400/week stipend
- Winner: OJT pays more

WORK EXPERIENCE:
- OJT: Real job, real experience, builds resume
- JRI: Training only, no work experience
- Winner: OJT

FLEXIBILITY:
- OJT: Must work employer's schedule
- JRI: More flexible, focus on training
- Winner: JRI

ELIGIBILITY:
- OJT: Anyone WIOA eligible
- JRI: Must have criminal justice involvement
- Winner: OJT (more people qualify)

LONG-TERM:
- OJT: Can lead to permanent job
- JRI: Ends when training ends
- Winner: OJT

SUPPORT:
- OJT: Employer supervision
- JRI: Weekly case management
- Winner: JRI (more support)

[SECTION 4: HOW TO DECIDE - 9:00-10:00]

Choose OJT if:
- You need maximum income
- You're ready to work now
- You want work experience
- You want a permanent job
- You can commit to employer schedule

Choose JRI if:
- You have criminal justice involvement
- You need more support
- You want to focus on training first
- You need flexibility
- You're not ready for full-time work yet

Can't decide? Talk to your case manager. They'll help you figure out what's best for your situation.

Remember: Both are great opportunities. Both can change your life. Choose the one that fits YOUR needs right now.

Let's get you earning while you learn!

[END]
