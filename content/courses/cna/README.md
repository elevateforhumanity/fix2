## Assessment Strategy

### Formative Assessments (Throughout Program)
- Knowledge check quizzes after each lesson
- Skills practice with instructor feedback
- Case study discussions
- Reflection assignments

### Summative Assessments (End of Modules)
- Module exams (written)
- Skills competency testing (hands-on)
- Clinical performance evaluations

### Final Assessments
- Comprehensive written exam (100 questions)
- Skills competency exam (demonstrate 5 randomly selected skills)
- Clinical evaluation (pass/fail based on performance)

## Clinical Requirements

### Clinical Site
- State-approved long-term care facility
- Minimum 80 hours supervised clinical experience
- Variety of patient assignments

### Clinical Schedule
- Typically 2-3 days per week
- 8-hour shifts
- Morning and evening shifts for diverse experience

### Clinical Expectations
- Professional appearance and behavior
- Punctuality and attendance
- Completion of assigned patient care
- Documentation of care provided
- Participation in shift report
- Demonstration of skills learned in lab
- Communication with instructor and facility staff

### Clinical Evaluation Criteria
- Safety (patient and self)
- Infection control practices
- Communication skills
- Technical skill performance
- Time management
- Professionalism
- Critical thinking
- Documentation

## Certification Exam Preparation

### State Exam Components

**Written Exam:**
- 60-100 multiple choice questions (varies by state)
- Topics: infection control, safety, patient rights, communication, basic nursing skills
- Passing score: typically 70-80%

**Skills Exam:**
- Demonstrate 5 randomly selected skills
- Performed on mannequin or volunteer
- Must complete all steps correctly
- Hand hygiene is always tested

### Exam Registration
- Students register after completing program
- Exam administered by state-approved testing agency
- Fee typically $100-150
- Results within 2-4 weeks

### Certification
- Upon passing both exams, student is certified CNA
- Certification valid for 2 years (varies by state)
- Must work minimum hours to maintain certification
- Renewal requires continuing education

## Program Completion Requirements

To successfully complete the CNA program, students must:
- [ ] Attend minimum 90% of classroom hours
- [ ] Complete all module assessments with 75% or higher
- [ ] Pass all skills competency tests
- [ ] Complete 80 hours of clinical experience
- [ ] Receive satisfactory clinical evaluation
- [ ] Pass comprehensive final exam with 75% or higher
- [ ] Demonstrate all required skills competencies

## Next Steps After Completion

1. **Register for State Exam**
   - Complete application
   - Pay exam fee
   - Schedule exam date

2. **Take State Certification Exam**
   - Written and skills components
   - Bring required identification

3. **Receive Certification**
   - Added to state registry
   - Can begin working as CNA

4. **Job Search**
   - Update resume
   - Apply to healthcare facilities
   - Attend job fairs
   - Network with clinical site contacts

5. **Continue Education**
   - Consider LPN or RN programs
   - Specialized certifications (medication aide, phlebotomy)
   - Continuing education for skill enhancement
