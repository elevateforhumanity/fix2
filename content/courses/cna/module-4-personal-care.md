# Module 4: Personal Care and Activities of Daily Living (ADLs)

## Overview
This module teaches essential personal care skills to help patients maintain hygiene, comfort, and dignity while performing activities of daily living.

**Duration:** 4 hours  
**Format:** Online reading + extensive hands-on skills lab  
**Prerequisites:** Modules 1-3 completed

---

## Lesson 4.1: Bathing and Hygiene

### Learning Objectives
- Perform complete and partial bed baths safely
- Assist with shower and tub baths
- Provide perineal care with dignity and respect
- Maintain patient privacy and comfort during bathing

### Importance of Bathing

**Benefits:**
- Removes dirt, sweat, and bacteria
- Prevents skin breakdown and infection
- Stimulates circulation
- Provides opportunity to observe skin condition
- Promotes relaxation and comfort
- Maintains dignity and self-esteem

**Types of Baths:**
- **Complete bed bath** - CNA washes entire body (patient cannot help)
- **Partial bed bath** - CNA washes face, hands, underarms, back, perineal area
- **Shower** - Patient showers with assistance
- **Tub bath** - Patient bathes in tub with assistance
- **Bag bath** - Pre-moistened disposable cloths (no rinse needed)

### Complete Bed Bath Procedure

**Supplies Needed:**
- Bath basin with warm water (105째F-110째F)
- Soap and washcloths (2-3)
- Bath towels (2-3)
- Bath blanket
- Clean gown
- Lotion
- Gloves
- Laundry bag

**Procedure:**

**Preparation:**
1. Perform hand hygiene
2. Gather supplies
3. Explain procedure to patient
4. Provide privacy (close door, pull curtain)
5. Raise bed to working height
6. Lower side rail on working side
7. Cover patient with bath blanket, remove top linens
8. Remove patient's gown (leave on if patient prefers until washing that area)
9. Fill basin with warm water (test temperature on inner wrist)

**Face and Neck:**
10. Ask if patient wants soap on face (many prefer water only)
11. Wash face with washcloth (no soap unless requested)
12. Wash from inner eye to outer eye (prevents infection)
13. Wash forehead, cheeks, nose, chin, ears, neck
14. Rinse and pat dry
15. Apply lotion if desired

**Arms and Hands:**
16. Uncover one arm (keep rest of body covered)
17. Place towel under arm
18. Wash arm from shoulder to wrist using long strokes
19. Wash hand and between fingers
20. Rinse and pat dry
21. Apply lotion
22. Repeat with other arm

**Chest and Abdomen:**
23. Uncover chest (keep breasts covered for female patients)
24. Place towel across chest
25. Wash chest using circular motions
26. For female patients, wash under breasts
27. Rinse and pat dry
28. Cover chest
29. Uncover abdomen
30. Wash abdomen using circular motions
31. Rinse and pat dry
32. Cover abdomen

**Legs and Feet:**
33. Uncover one leg (keep other leg and perineal area covered)
34. Place towel under leg
35. Wash leg from hip to ankle using long strokes
36. Wash foot, between toes, and under toenails
37. Rinse and pat dry
38. Apply lotion
39. Repeat with other leg

**Back:**
40. Turn patient on side (facing away from you)
41. Place towel along back
42. Wash back from neck to buttocks using long strokes
43. Rinse and pat dry
44. Apply lotion and give back massage if desired
45. Observe skin for redness or breakdown

**Perineal Care:**
46. Put on gloves
47. Provide privacy
48. Position patient on back
49. Drape with bath blanket (diamond shape, one corner at chest, corners at sides, one corner between legs)
50. Change water and washcloth
51. Perform perineal care (see detailed procedure below)
52. Remove gloves, perform hand hygiene

**Completion:**
53. Help patient put on clean gown
54. Change bed linens if needed
55. Position patient comfortably
56. Lower bed
57. Raise side rails if needed
58. Place call light within reach
59. Open curtain/door if patient prefers
60. Empty and clean basin
61. Place soiled linens in laundry bag
62. Perform hand hygiene
63. Document bath given and skin condition

### Perineal Care (Peri-Care)

**Female Perineal Care:**
1. Put on gloves
2. Position patient on back with knees bent and separated
3. Drape for privacy
4. Apply soap to washcloth
5. Separate labia with one hand
6. Wash from front to back (prevents infection)
7. Use different area of cloth for each stroke
8. Wash outer labia, then inner labia
9. Rinse thoroughly from front to back
10. Pat dry
11. Turn patient on side
12. Wash anal area from front to back
13. Rinse and pat dry
14. Remove gloves, perform hand hygiene

**Male Perineal Care:**
1. Put on gloves
2. Position patient on back
3. Drape for privacy
4. Apply soap to washcloth
5. If uncircumcised, gently retract foreskin
6. Wash tip of penis using circular motion
7. Wash shaft of penis from tip to base
8. Rinse thoroughly
9. If uncircumcised, return foreskin to normal position (prevents swelling)
10. Wash scrotum and groin area
11. Pat dry
12. Turn patient on side
13. Wash anal area from front to back
14. Rinse and pat dry
15. Remove gloves, perform hand hygiene

**Important:**
- Always wash from clean to dirty (front to back)
- Use different area of cloth for each stroke
- Be professional and matter-of-fact
- Provide privacy and maintain dignity
- Observe for redness, swelling, discharge, odor
- Report abnormalities to nurse

### Shower and Tub Bath Assistance

**Shower Assistance:**
1. Ensure bathroom is warm and safe
2. Place non-slip mat in shower
3. Provide shower chair if needed
4. Help patient to bathroom
5. Stay nearby (outside curtain for privacy)
6. Assist with washing hard-to-reach areas if needed
7. Help patient dry off
8. Assist with dressing
9. Help patient back to room
10. Clean shower area

**Tub Bath Assistance:**
1. Fill tub with warm water (check temperature)
2. Place non-slip mat in tub
3. Help patient into tub (use transfer techniques)
4. Never leave patient alone in tub
5. Assist with washing if needed
6. Drain tub before helping patient out
7. Help patient stand and dry off
8. Assist with dressing
9. Help patient back to room
10. Clean tub

**Safety Considerations:**
- Check water temperature (105째F-110째F)
- Use non-slip mats
- Install grab bars
- Never leave patient alone
- Keep call light within reach
- Ensure adequate lighting
- Remove electrical appliances from bathroom
- Keep floor dry

## Lesson 4.2: Oral Care and Grooming

### Oral Care

**Importance:**
- Prevents tooth decay and gum disease
- Prevents bad breath
- Prevents infection (especially pneumonia)
- Promotes comfort and dignity
- Stimulates appetite

**Oral Care for Conscious Patient:**

**Supplies:**
- Toothbrush and toothpaste
- Cup of water
- Emesis basin
- Towel
- Gloves
- Lip moisturizer

**Procedure:**
1. Perform hand hygiene, gather supplies
2. Explain procedure
3. Raise head of bed (45-90 degrees)
4. Place towel across chest
5. Put on gloves
6. Apply toothpaste to toothbrush
7. Hand toothbrush to patient or brush teeth for them
8. Brush all surfaces of teeth (front, back, chewing surfaces)
9. Brush tongue
10. Offer water to rinse
11. Hold emesis basin for patient to spit
12. Wipe mouth with towel
13. Apply lip moisturizer
14. Remove gloves, perform hand hygiene
15. Document oral care provided

**Oral Care for Unconscious Patient:**

**Special Considerations:**
- Risk of aspiration (fluid entering lungs)
- Cannot swallow or spit
- Position on side to prevent choking
- Use minimal water
- Use foam swabs instead of toothbrush

**Procedure:**
1. Perform hand hygiene, gather supplies
2. Explain procedure (even if unconscious)
3. Position patient on side
4. Place towel under head
5. Put on gloves
6. Use foam swab dipped in mouthwash or water
7. Gently clean teeth, gums, tongue, inside of cheeks
8. Use minimal moisture
9. Wipe mouth with towel
10. Apply lip moisturizer
11. Remove gloves, perform hand hygiene
12. Document oral care provided

**Never use toothbrush on unconscious patient - risk of choking**

### Denture Care

**Procedure:**
1. Perform hand hygiene, gather supplies
2. Put on gloves
3. Ask patient to remove dentures or remove for them
4. Place towel in sink (prevents breaking if dropped)
5. Rinse dentures under cool running water
6. Apply denture cleaner or toothpaste
7. Brush all surfaces
8. Rinse thoroughly under cool water
9. Return dentures to patient or store in denture cup with water
10. Help patient rinse mouth
11. Remove gloves, perform hand hygiene

**Important:**
- Handle dentures carefully (expensive to replace)
- Use cool water (hot water can warp dentures)
- Store in water (prevents drying and cracking)
- Label denture cup with patient's name
- Never wrap dentures in tissue (may be thrown away)

### Hair Care

**Shampooing in Bed:**
1. Gather supplies (shampoo, water, basin, towels, plastic sheet)
2. Explain procedure
3. Remove pillow, place plastic sheet under head
4. Place basin under head to catch water
5. Wet hair with warm water
6. Apply shampoo and massage scalp
7. Rinse thoroughly
8. Apply conditioner if desired, rinse
9. Wrap hair in towel
10. Dry hair with towel or blow dryer
11. Comb or brush hair gently
12. Style as patient prefers

**Combing and Brushing:**
- Brush or comb daily
- Start at ends and work toward scalp (prevents pulling)
- Be gentle with tangles
- Use detangler spray if needed
- Style as patient prefers
- For African American hair, use wide-tooth comb or pick
- Braid long hair to prevent tangling

### Shaving

**Safety Razor Shaving:**
1. Perform hand hygiene, gather supplies
2. Put on gloves
3. Apply warm, moist towel to face (softens beard)
4. Apply shaving cream
5. Hold skin taut
6. Shave in direction of hair growth
7. Use short, gentle strokes
8. Rinse razor frequently
9. Rinse face with warm water
10. Pat dry
11. Apply aftershave or lotion if desired
12. Remove gloves, perform hand hygiene

**Electric Razor Shaving:**
1. Perform hand hygiene
2. Ensure face is dry
3. Turn on razor
4. Shave in circular motion or direction of hair growth
5. Clean razor after use
6. Apply aftershave or lotion if desired
7. Perform hand hygiene

**Important:**
- Never use safety razor on patients taking blood thinners (risk of bleeding)
- Use electric razor for patients on blood thinners
- Check facility policy on who can shave patients
- Some facilities only allow electric razors

### Nail Care

**Fingernail Care:**
1. Soak hands in warm water for 5-10 minutes
2. Clean under nails with orangewood stick
3. Trim nails straight across with nail clippers
4. File nails smooth
5. Apply lotion to hands

**Toenail Care:**
1. Soak feet in warm water for 10-15 minutes
2. Clean under nails
3. Trim nails straight across (prevents ingrown toenails)
4. File nails smooth
5. Apply lotion to feet (not between toes)

**Important:**
- Never cut nails of diabetic patients (risk of infection)
- Report thick, discolored, or ingrown nails to nurse
- Check facility policy on nail care
- Some facilities require nurse or podiatrist to trim nails

## Lesson 4.3: Dressing and Toileting

### Dressing

**General Principles:**
- Encourage independence
- Allow patient to choose clothing
- Provide privacy
- Dress affected (weak) side first
- Undress unaffected (strong) side first
- Use adaptive clothing if needed (Velcro, elastic waists)

**Putting on Shirt/Gown:**
1. Gather clothing
2. Explain procedure
3. Provide privacy
4. Position patient sitting if possible
5. Unbutton or untie gown
6. Remove from strong arm first
7. Remove from weak arm
8. Put clean gown on weak arm first
9. Put on strong arm
10. Button or tie gown
11. Ensure patient is covered and comfortable

**Putting on Pants:**
1. Have patient lie down
2. Gather pants at waist
3. Slide pants over weak leg first
4. Slide pants over strong leg
5. Pull pants up to hips
6. Have patient lift hips or roll side to side
7. Pull pants up to waist
8. Fasten pants

**Putting on Socks and Shoes:**
1. Put sock on weak foot first
2. Put sock on strong foot
3. Put shoe on weak foot first
4. Put shoe on strong foot
5. Tie or fasten shoes

**Removing Clothing:**
- Remove from strong side first
- Remove from weak side last
- Support weak limbs
- Never force movement

### Toileting Assistance

**Using Toilet:**
1. Respond to request promptly
2. Help patient to bathroom
3. Help lower pants
4. Help patient sit on toilet
5. Provide privacy (stay nearby)
6. Provide toilet paper
7. Help with wiping if needed (front to back for females)
8. Help patient stand
9. Help pull up pants
10. Assist with hand washing
11. Help patient back to bed/chair
12. Perform hand hygiene

**Using Bedside Commode:**
1. Place commode next to bed
2. Lock wheels
3. Help patient sit on commode
4. Provide privacy
5. Provide toilet paper
6. Help with wiping if needed
7. Help patient back to bed
8. Cover and remove commode container
9. Empty, rinse, and disinfect container
10. Return commode to bedside
11. Perform hand hygiene

**Using Bedpan:**

**Types:**
- **Regular bedpan** - For most patients
- **Fracture pan** - Smaller, for patients who cannot lift hips

**Procedure:**
1. Warm bedpan with warm water, dry
2. Put on gloves
3. Lower head of bed (flat or slightly elevated)
4. Help patient bend knees and lift hips
5. Slide bedpan under buttocks (wide end toward back)
6. Or roll patient on side, place bedpan, roll back onto pan
7. Raise head of bed to comfortable position
8. Provide privacy
9. Place toilet paper and call light within reach
10. Leave room if safe to do so
11. Return when called
12. Lower head of bed
13. Help patient lift hips, remove bedpan
14. Provide perineal care if needed
15. Cover bedpan, take to bathroom
16. Observe contents (amount, color, consistency)
17. Empty, rinse, and disinfect bedpan
18. Remove gloves, perform hand hygiene
19. Help patient wash hands
20. Document elimination

**Using Urinal (Male):**
1. Provide urinal
2. Help position if needed
3. Provide privacy
4. Return when called
5. Put on gloves
6. Remove urinal
7. Observe contents
8. Empty, rinse, and disinfect urinal
9. Remove gloves, perform hand hygiene
10. Help patient wash hands
11. Document elimination

### Incontinence Care

**Types of Incontinence:**
- **Urinary incontinence** - Loss of bladder control
- **Bowel incontinence** - Loss of bowel control
- **Functional incontinence** - Cannot get to toilet in time
- **Stress incontinence** - Leakage with coughing, sneezing, laughing
- **Urge incontinence** - Sudden, strong need to urinate

**Incontinence Care:**
1. Respond promptly to requests
2. Offer toileting every 2 hours
3. Limit fluids before bedtime (not during day)
4. Provide easy access to bathroom
5. Use incontinence products (briefs, pads)
6. Change wet or soiled products immediately
7. Provide thorough perineal care
8. Apply barrier cream to prevent skin breakdown
9. Maintain dignity and respect
10. Never scold or shame patient

**Changing Incontinence Brief:**
1. Gather supplies (clean brief, wipes, barrier cream, gloves)
2. Provide privacy
3. Put on gloves
4. Turn patient on side
5. Unfasten soiled brief
6. Roll brief inward (contains waste)
7. Clean perineal area thoroughly (front to back)
8. Apply barrier cream
9. Position clean brief under patient
10. Turn patient onto back and over to other side
11. Pull brief through and fasten
12. Ensure brief fits snugly but not too tight
13. Dispose of soiled brief in appropriate container
14. Remove gloves, perform hand hygiene
15. Position patient comfortably
16. Document care provided

## Module 4 Assessment

### Case Studies

**Case Study 1:**
Mrs. Garcia is 78 years old and had a stroke affecting her right side. She needs a complete bed bath. How will you modify the procedure for her?

**Answer:** Wash and dress her weak (right) side first. Support her right arm and leg during bathing. Be extra gentle with affected side. Allow extra time. Encourage her to help with her strong (left) side.

**Case Study 2:**
Mr. Johnson is unconscious and on a ventilator. How will you provide oral care?

**Answer:** Position on side to prevent aspiration. Use foam swabs instead of toothbrush. Use minimal moisture. Clean teeth, gums, tongue, and inside of cheeks. Apply lip moisturizer. Never use toothbrush on unconscious patient.

**Case Study 3:**
Ms. Lee is incontinent of urine and has developed a red, irritated area on her buttocks. What should you do?

**Answer:** Report skin breakdown to nurse immediately. Change incontinence brief immediately when wet. Provide thorough perineal care. Apply barrier cream. Reposition frequently. Keep skin clean and dry. Document skin condition.

## Key Takeaways

- Personal care maintains hygiene, comfort, and dignity
- Always provide privacy and respect
- Encourage independence when possible
- Observe skin condition during care
- Report abnormalities to nurse
- Document all care provided
- Safety is priority during bathing and toileting
