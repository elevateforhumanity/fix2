# Module 7: Restorative Care

## Overview
Restorative care focuses on helping patients regain and maintain their highest level of independence and function after illness, injury, or surgery.

**Duration:** 2 hours  
**Format:** Online reading + practical exercises  
**Prerequisites:** Modules 1-6 completed

---

## Learning Objectives
- Understand principles of restorative care
- Encourage patient independence in ADLs
- Perform range of motion exercises correctly
- Assist with bowel and bladder training programs
- Support use of prosthetic and assistive devices

---

## Lesson 7.1: Principles of Restorative Care

### What is Restorative Care?

**Definition:** Care that helps patients achieve and maintain their highest possible level of function and independence.

**Goals:**
- Prevent decline in function
- Restore lost abilities
- Maintain current abilities
- Promote independence
- Improve quality of life
- Prevent complications

### Philosophy of Restorative Care

**Key Principles:**

1. **Focus on Abilities, Not Disabilities**
   - Emphasize what patient CAN do
   - Build on strengths
   - Celebrate small victories

2. **Encourage Independence**
   - Allow extra time for tasks
   - Resist urge to "do it for them"
   - Provide encouragement and praise

3. **Individualized Care**
   - Each patient has unique needs
   - Set realistic, achievable goals
   - Adjust approach as needed

4. **Team Approach**
   - Work with PT, OT, nurses
   - Follow care plan consistently
   - Communicate progress

5. **Patience and Persistence**
   - Progress may be slow
   - Setbacks are normal
   - Stay positive and encouraging

### Benefits of Restorative Care

**For Patients:**
- Increased independence
- Improved self-esteem
- Better quality of life
- Reduced risk of complications
- Faster recovery
- Greater satisfaction

**For Facilities:**
- Better patient outcomes
- Reduced hospital readmissions
- Lower healthcare costs
- Improved satisfaction scores
- Regulatory compliance

### CNA's Role in Restorative Care

**Your Responsibilities:**
- Follow care plan exactly
- Encourage patient participation
- Allow adequate time
- Provide positive reinforcement
- Report progress to nurse
- Document activities
- Never do for patient what they can do themselves

## Lesson 7.2: Encouraging Independence in ADLs

### Bathing

**Restorative Approach:**
- Let patient wash face and hands
- Patient washes reachable areas
- You wash hard-to-reach areas
- Provide adaptive equipment (long-handled sponge, shower chair)
- Give step-by-step instructions
- Allow extra time

**Example:**
"Mrs. Jones, let's wash your face first. Here's the washcloth. Great job! Now let's do your arms..."

### Dressing

**Restorative Approach:**
- Lay out clothes in order
- Patient dresses upper body (if able)
- Use adaptive clothing (Velcro, elastic)
- Teach techniques (dress weak side first)
- Provide encouragement
- Allow 20-30 minutes

**Adaptive Techniques:**
- Button hooks for buttons
- Zipper pulls for zippers
- Elastic shoelaces
- Slip-on shoes
- Front-closing garments

### Eating

**Restorative Approach:**
- Position upright
- Provide adaptive utensils
- Cut food if needed
- Patient feeds self
- Encourage use of affected hand
- Allow 45-60 minutes for meals

**Adaptive Equipment:**
- Built-up handles on utensils
- Plate guards
- Non-slip mats
- Cups with lids and spouts
- Weighted utensils (for tremors)

### Toileting

**Restorative Approach:**
- Establish regular schedule
- Patient does as much as possible
- Provide grab bars
- Use raised toilet seat
- Encourage independence
- Assist only as needed

### Grooming

**Restorative Approach:**
- Patient brushes teeth
- Patient combs hair
- Patient shaves (electric razor)
- Provide adaptive equipment
- Give verbal cues
- Praise efforts

## Lesson 7.3: Range of Motion Exercises

### Types of ROM Exercises

**Active ROM:**
- Patient moves joints independently
- No assistance needed
- Best for maintaining function

**Active-Assistive ROM:**
- Patient initiates movement
- You provide some assistance
- Helps build strength

**Passive ROM:**
- You move joints for patient
- Patient cannot move independently
- Prevents contractures

### ROM Exercise Guidelines

**General Rules:**
- Support joint being exercised
- Move slowly and gently
- Stop if patient reports pain
- Never force movement
- Repeat each movement 3-5 times
- Exercise both sides of body
- Perform daily (or per care plan)

### Upper Body ROM Exercises

**Shoulder:**
1. **Flexion/Extension**
   - Raise arm forward and up
   - Return to side
   
2. **Abduction/Adduction**
   - Raise arm out to side
   - Return to side
   
3. **Internal/External Rotation**
   - Bend elbow 90 degrees
   - Rotate forearm in and out

**Elbow:**
1. **Flexion/Extension**
   - Bend elbow (hand to shoulder)
   - Straighten elbow

**Wrist:**
1. **Flexion/Extension**
   - Bend wrist down
   - Bend wrist up
   
2. **Radial/Ulnar Deviation**
   - Bend wrist toward thumb
   - Bend wrist toward little finger

**Fingers:**
1. **Flexion/Extension**
   - Make fist
   - Straighten fingers
   
2. **Abduction/Adduction**
   - Spread fingers apart
   - Bring fingers together

### Lower Body ROM Exercises

**Hip:**
1. **Flexion/Extension**
   - Bend knee toward chest
   - Straighten leg
   
2. **Abduction/Adduction**
   - Move leg out to side
   - Return to center
   
3. **Internal/External Rotation**
   - Roll leg inward
   - Roll leg outward

**Knee:**
1. **Flexion/Extension**
   - Bend knee
   - Straighten knee

**Ankle:**
1. **Dorsiflexion/Plantar Flexion**
   - Pull toes toward shin
   - Point toes down
   
2. **Inversion/Eversion**
   - Turn sole inward
   - Turn sole outward

**Toes:**
1. **Flexion/Extension**
   - Curl toes down
   - Straighten toes

## Lesson 7.4: Bowel and Bladder Training

### Bowel Training Program

**Purpose:** Establish regular bowel elimination pattern

**Components:**

1. **Regular Schedule**
   - Same time each day (usually after breakfast)
   - Take advantage of gastrocolic reflex
   - Allow 15-30 minutes

2. **Proper Positioning**
   - Sitting position (on toilet or commode)
   - Feet flat on floor or stool
   - Lean slightly forward

3. **Privacy**
   - Close door/curtain
   - Leave room if safe
   - Provide call light

4. **Diet**
   - High fiber foods
   - Adequate fluids (8 cups/day)
   - Prune juice if ordered

5. **Activity**
   - Regular exercise
   - Ambulation
   - Abdominal exercises

6. **Documentation**
   - Record bowel movements
   - Note consistency, amount
   - Report problems

### Bladder Training Program

**Purpose:** Establish regular voiding pattern, reduce incontinence

**Components:**

1. **Scheduled Toileting**
   - Every 2 hours while awake
   - Before bed and upon waking
   - After meals
   - Gradually increase intervals

2. **Fluid Management**
   - Adequate fluids during day (8 cups)
   - Limit fluids 2-3 hours before bed
   - Avoid caffeine and alcohol

3. **Pelvic Floor Exercises (Kegels)**
   - Tighten pelvic muscles
   - Hold 5-10 seconds
   - Relax
   - Repeat 10 times, 3x daily

4. **Proper Positioning**
   - Sitting position for women
   - Standing or sitting for men
   - Privacy and comfort

5. **Positive Reinforcement**
   - Praise successes
   - Don't scold for accidents
   - Encourage participation

6. **Documentation**
   - Record voiding times
   - Note amount (small, moderate, large)
   - Document accidents
   - Report patterns

## Lesson 7.5: Prosthetic and Assistive Devices

### Prosthetic Devices

**Types:**
- Artificial limbs (arms, legs)
- Artificial eyes
- Dentures (already covered)

**CNA's Role with Prosthetics:**

**Artificial Limbs:**
- Inspect stump for redness, swelling, breakdown
- Keep stump clean and dry
- Apply stump sock if used
- Help apply prosthetic
- Check fit (should be snug but not tight)
- Report problems to nurse
- Encourage use during ADLs

**Care of Prosthetic:**
- Clean according to manufacturer instructions
- Check for damage or wear
- Store safely when not in use
- Never try to repair yourself

### Assistive Devices for ADLs

**Dressing Aids:**
- Button hooks
- Zipper pulls
- Sock aids
- Long-handled shoehorns
- Elastic shoelaces
- Reacher/grabber

**Eating Aids:**
- Built-up utensil handles
- Weighted utensils
- Swivel utensils
- Plate guards
- Non-slip mats
- Cups with handles/lids

**Bathing Aids:**
- Long-handled sponges
- Shower chairs/benches
- Grab bars
- Non-slip mats
- Hand-held shower heads

**Toileting Aids:**
- Raised toilet seats
- Toilet safety frames
- Grab bars
- Bedside commodes

**Mobility Aids:**
- Walkers
- Canes
- Crutches
- Wheelchairs
- (Covered in Module 6)

### Orthotic Devices

**Definition:** Devices that support or correct body alignment

**Types:**
- Braces (leg, back, neck)
- Splints (hand, wrist, ankle)
- Foot orthotics (shoe inserts)

**CNA's Role:**
- Help apply/remove as ordered
- Check skin under device for redness
- Keep device clean
- Report problems
- Encourage proper use

## Key Takeaways

- Restorative care helps patients achieve maximum independence
- Focus on abilities, not disabilities
- Allow extra time for patients to do tasks themselves
