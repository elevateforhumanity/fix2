# HVAC Technician Program Course Content

## Program Overview
Complete HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning) Technician training program preparing students for EPA 608 certification and entry-level HVAC careers.

**Total Hours:** 240 hours  
**Duration:** 12-16 weeks  
**Credential:** EPA 608 Universal Certification + OSHA 10  
**Delivery:** Hybrid (classroom + hands-on lab)

## Program Modules

### Module 1: HVAC Fundamentals (20 hours)
- Introduction to HVAC systems
- Career pathways and opportunities
- Safety in HVAC work
- Tools and equipment overview
- Basic electrical theory
- Blueprint reading basics

### Module 2: Heating Systems (30 hours)
- Gas furnaces
- Oil furnaces
- Electric heating systems
- Heat pumps
- Boilers and hydronic systems
- Troubleshooting heating systems

### Module 3: Cooling Systems (30 hours)
- Refrigeration cycle
- Air conditioning components
- Central AC systems
- Ductless mini-splits
- Evaporative coolers
- Troubleshooting cooling systems

### Module 4: Refrigeration Principles (25 hours)
- Properties of refrigerants
- Pressure-temperature relationships
- Refrigerant handling and recovery
- EPA 608 certification prep
- Environmental regulations
- Refrigerant safety

### Module 5: Electrical Systems (25 hours)
- Electrical circuits and components
- Reading wiring diagrams
- Multimeter usage
- Thermostats and controls
- Motor types and troubleshooting
- Electrical safety

### Module 6: Ventilation and Air Quality (20 hours)
- Ventilation principles
- Ductwork design and installation
- Air filtration systems
- Indoor air quality
- Energy efficiency
- Building codes and standards

### Module 7: System Installation (30 hours)
- Equipment sizing and selection
- Installation procedures
- Ductwork installation
- Refrigerant line installation
- Startup and commissioning
- Quality control

### Module 8: Maintenance and Troubleshooting (30 hours)
- Preventive maintenance procedures
- Diagnostic techniques
- Common problems and solutions
- Customer service skills
- Service call procedures
- Documentation and reporting

### Module 9: Business and Customer Service (15 hours)
- HVAC business operations
- Estimating and bidding
- Customer communication
- Sales techniques
- Time management
- Professional ethics

### Module 10: Certification Preparation (15 hours)
- EPA 608 exam review (Core, Type I, II, III)
- OSHA 10 certification
- Practice exams
- Hands-on skills assessment
- Job search preparation

## Required Certifications

### EPA 608 Universal Certification
**Required for:** Handling refrigerants  
**Sections:**
- Core (required for all)
- Type I (small appliances)
- Type II (high-pressure systems)
- Type III (low-pressure systems)
- Universal (all four sections)

**Exam Format:** 100 multiple choice questions  
**Passing Score:** 70% on each section

### OSHA 10-Hour Construction Safety
**Topics:**
- Fall protection
- Electrical safety
- Personal protective equipment
- Hazard communication
- Ladder safety
- Tool safety

## Skills Competencies

Students must demonstrate:
- [ ] Safe use of HVAC tools
- [ ] Refrigerant recovery and charging
- [ ] Electrical troubleshooting with multimeter
- [ ] Thermostat installation and wiring
- [ ] Ductwork fabrication and installation
- [ ] System startup and commissioning
- [ ] Preventive maintenance procedures
- [ ] Leak detection and repair
- [ ] Brazing and soldering
- [ ] Customer service and communication

## Career Pathways

**Entry-Level:**
- HVAC Technician Helper
- Residential HVAC Technician
- Commercial HVAC Technician
- Maintenance Technician

**Advanced:**
- Lead HVAC Technician
- HVAC Service Manager
- HVAC Installer
- Controls Specialist
- Energy Auditor

**Entrepreneurship:**
- HVAC Contractor (requires additional licensing)
- HVAC Consulting
- Specialty Services (duct cleaning, indoor air quality)

## Earning Potential
- Entry-level: $35,000-45,000/year
- Experienced: $50,000-70,000/year
- Master technician: $70,000-90,000+/year

## Tools Required
- Multimeter
- Manifold gauge set
- Refrigerant recovery machine (provided by employer)
- Hand tools (screwdrivers, wrenches, pliers)
- Power tools (drill, reciprocating saw)
- Safety equipment (gloves, safety glasses, respirator)

**Estimated Cost:** $500-1,000 (basic tool kit)
