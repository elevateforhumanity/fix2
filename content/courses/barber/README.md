# Barber Apprenticeship Program Course Content

## Program Overview
Complete Barber Apprenticeship program with 1,500 hours of training combining classroom instruction, skills lab practice, and on-the-job training in licensed barbershops.

## Program Structure

**Total Hours:** 1,500 hours
- **Related Instruction (Classroom):** 300 hours
- **Practical Training (Barbershop):** 1,200 hours

**Duration:** 12-18 months (depending on schedule)

**Credential:** State Barber License (Apprenticeship Pathway)

## Apprenticeship Requirements

### Eligibility
- Minimum age 16 (varies by state)
- High school diploma or GED
- Valid government-issued ID
- Pass background check (if required by state)

### Apprenticeship Agreement
- Registered with State Board of Barbers
- Signed agreement between apprentice, mentor barber, and shop owner
- Minimum 1,500 hours (varies by state)
- Regular progress reports
- Mentor barber must hold active license for minimum 3 years

### Work Schedule
- Minimum 20 hours per week in barbershop
- Maximum 40 hours per week
- Classroom instruction typically 1 day per week
- Duration: 12-18 months

### Compensation
- Apprentices may receive wages (varies by shop)
- Tips allowed in most states
- Some shops offer commission after certain milestones

### Supervision Requirements
- Must work under licensed barber at all times
- Mentor barber must be present in shop
- Cannot work independently until licensed
- Mentor reviews and signs off on all services

## State Licensing Exam

### Written Exam
**Format:** 100 multiple choice questions  
**Topics:**
- Sanitation and safety (30%)
- Hair cutting and styling (25%)
- Chemical services (20%)
- State laws and regulations (15%)
- Business practices (10%)

**Passing Score:** 75% (varies by state)

### Practical Exam
**Format:** Demonstrate skills on live model or mannequin

**Required Skills (varies by state):**
1. Sanitation setup
2. Haircut (clipper and shear)
3. Fade or taper
4. Beard trim
5. Straight razor shave
6. Chemical service (color or relaxer)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Safety and sanitation
- Technique and precision
- Time management
- Client communication
- Final result quality

### Exam Registration
- Apply after completing 1,500 hours
- Submit proof of apprenticeship hours
- Pay exam fee ($100-200, varies by state)
- Schedule exam date
- Bring required tools and supplies
