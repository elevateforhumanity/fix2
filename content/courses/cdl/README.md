# Commercial Driver's License (CDL) Class A Program

## Program Overview
Complete CDL Class A training program preparing students for commercial truck driving careers with emphasis on safety, regulations, and professional driving skills.

**Total Hours:** 160 hours  
**Duration:** 4-6 weeks  
**Credential:** CDL Class A License  
**Delivery:** Hybrid (classroom + behind-the-wheel training)

## Program Modules

### Module 1: Introduction to Commercial Driving (10 hours)
- Commercial driving career overview
- Types of commercial vehicles and endorsements
- CDL licensing process and requirements
- Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations (FMCSR)
- Hours of Service (HOS) regulations
- Driver qualification requirements
- Professional driver responsibilities

### Module 2: Vehicle Systems and Inspection (20 hours)
- Truck components and systems
- Engine, transmission, and drivetrain
- Brake systems (air brakes)
- Steering and suspension
- Electrical systems
- Pre-trip inspection procedures
- Post-trip inspection
- Identifying and reporting defects
- Vehicle maintenance basics

### Module 3: Basic Vehicle Control (25 hours)
- Starting and stopping
- Shifting gears (manual transmission)
- Steering techniques
- Backing maneuvers
- Straight line backing
- Offset backing
- Parallel parking
- Alley dock backing
- Coupling and uncoupling trailers

### Module 4: Safe Driving Practices (20 hours)
- Defensive driving techniques
- Space management
- Speed management
- Night driving
- Adverse weather driving
- Mountain driving
- Hazard perception
- Emergency maneuvers
- Accident procedures

### Module 5: Air Brakes (15 hours)
- Air brake system components
- Air brake operation
- Inspecting air brakes
- Using air brakes safely
- Emergency braking
- Air brake endorsement test preparation

### Module 6: Cargo Handling (10 hours)
- Cargo securement regulations
- Load distribution and weight limits
- Securing different types of cargo
- Flatbed securement
- Tanker considerations
- Hazardous materials overview
- Documentation and paperwork

### Module 7: Transportation Regulations (15 hours)
- Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations
- Hours of Service rules
- Electronic Logging Devices (ELD)
- Drug and alcohol testing requirements
- Medical certification requirements
- Roadside inspections
- CSA (Compliance, Safety, Accountability) program
- State-specific regulations

### Module 8: Behind-the-Wheel Training (40 hours)
- Supervised driving on public roads
- City driving
- Highway driving
- Rural road driving
- Various traffic conditions
- Real-world scenarios
- Skills development and refinement
- Test route practice

### Module 9: CDL Test Preparation (5 hours)
- Written knowledge test review
- Skills test preparation
- Pre-trip inspection practice
- Backing skills practice
- Road test preparation
- Test-taking strategies
- Mock tests

## CDL Testing Requirements

### Knowledge Tests (Written)
**General Knowledge Test** - Required for all CDL applicants  
**Air Brakes Test** - Required for air brake endorsement  
**Combination Vehicles Test** - Required for Class A

**Optional Endorsements:**
- Hazardous Materials (HazMat)
- Tanker Vehicles
- Doubles/Triples
- Passenger Transport
- School Bus

### Skills Tests (Practical)

**Pre-Trip Inspection**
- Demonstrate thorough vehicle inspection
- Identify components and explain function
- Identify potential defects
- Time limit: 30-60 minutes

**Basic Vehicle Control**
- Straight line backing
- Offset backing (left and right)
- Parallel parking (driver side and conventional)
- Alley dock backing
- Scored on accuracy and number of pull-ups

**Road Test**
- Left and right turns
- Intersections
- Railroad crossings
- Curves
- Upgrade and downgrade
- Expressway/freeway driving
- Lane changes
- Traffic circles
- Scored on safety and proper technique

## Skills Competencies

Students must demonstrate:
- [ ] Complete pre-trip inspection
- [ ] Identify vehicle defects

- [ ] Execute backing maneuvers accurately
- [ ] Couple and uncouple trailer
- [ ] Perform air brake checks
- [ ] Navigate turns safely
- [ ] Maintain proper following distance
- [ ] Use mirrors effectively
- [ ] Manage speed appropriately
- [ ] Handle emergency situations
- [ ] Complete trip documentation
- [ ] Communicate with dispatch

## Medical Requirements

### DOT Physical Exam
Required for all CDL holders:
- Vision: 20/40 in each eye (with or without correction)
- Hearing: Able to hear forced whisper at 5 feet
- Blood pressure: Below 140/90 (may require monitoring)
- No disqualifying medical conditions
- Valid for up to 2 years

### Drug and Alcohol Testing
- Pre-employment testing required
- Random testing throughout employment
- Post-accident testing
- Reasonable suspicion testing
- Return-to-duty testing
- Zero tolerance policy
