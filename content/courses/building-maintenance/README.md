# Building Maintenance Technician Program

## Program Overview
Complete Building Maintenance Technician training program preparing students for careers in facility maintenance, property management, and building operations.

**Total Hours:** 200 hours  
**Duration:** 10-12 weeks  
**Credentials:** OSHA 10, EPA 608 (Type I), Multiple skill certifications  
**Delivery:** Hybrid (classroom + hands-on lab)

## Program Modules

### Module 1: Introduction to Building Maintenance (15 hours)
- Career overview and opportunities
- Types of facilities (commercial, residential, industrial, institutional)
- Maintenance technician responsibilities
- Safety culture and workplace safety
- Tools and equipment overview
- Work order systems
- Customer service fundamentals
- Professional conduct

### Module 2: Safety and OSHA Compliance (20 hours)
- OSHA 10-Hour General Industry certification
- Personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures
- Fall protection
- Ladder safety
- Confined space awareness
- Hazard communication
- Emergency procedures
- Incident reporting
- Material Safety Data Sheets (SDS)

### Module 3: Basic Electrical Systems (25 hours)
- Electrical safety
- Electrical theory basics
- Reading electrical diagrams
- Circuit breakers and fuses
- Outlets and switches
- Lighting systems (LED, fluorescent, incandescent)
- Ballast replacement
- Basic troubleshooting
- Multimeter usage
- When to call an electrician

### Module 4: Plumbing Systems (25 hours)
- Plumbing safety
- Water supply systems
- Drain, waste, and vent systems
- Fixtures (toilets, sinks, faucets)
- Common plumbing repairs
- Leak detection and repair
- Drain cleaning
- Water heater maintenance
- Backflow prevention
- When to call a plumber

### Module 5: HVAC Basics (20 hours)
- HVAC system overview
- Heating systems
- Cooling systems
- Ventilation and air quality
- Filter replacement
- Thermostat operation
- Basic troubleshooting
- Preventive maintenance
- EPA 608 Type I certification (small appliances)
- When to call HVAC technician

### Module 6: Carpentry and Drywall (20 hours)
- Hand and power tool safety
- Measuring and layout
- Basic framing
- Door and window repair
- Drywall repair and patching
- Painting and finishing
- Trim and molding installation
- Cabinet repair
- Flooring basics
- Hardware installation

### Module 7: Preventive Maintenance (15 hours)
- Preventive maintenance principles
- Creating maintenance schedules
- Equipment inspection procedures
- Lubrication and cleaning
- Filter replacement schedules
- Seasonal maintenance tasks
- Record keeping and documentation
- Computerized Maintenance Management Systems (CMMS)
- Inventory management
- Vendor coordination

### Module 8: Grounds and Exterior Maintenance (15 hours)
- Landscaping basics
- Lawn care and irrigation
- Snow and ice removal
- Parking lot maintenance
- Exterior lighting
- Building envelope inspection
- Roof basics and leak detection
- Gutter cleaning
- Pest control awareness
- Exterior painting

### Module 9: Specialized Systems (20 hours)
- Fire alarm systems
- Security systems
- Access control systems
- Emergency lighting
- Elevator basics (awareness only)
- Boiler operation basics
- Pool and spa maintenance (if applicable)
- Generator operation
- Backflow testing
- Water treatment

### Module 10: Customer Service and Communication (15 hours)
- Professional communication
- Handling tenant/resident requests
- Managing difficult situations
- Time management and prioritization
- Work order management
- Documentation and reporting
- Team collaboration
- Vendor communication
- Emergency response protocols
- Continuous improvement

### Module 11: Job Readiness and Certification Prep (10 hours)
- Resume building
- Interview skills
- Job search strategies
- Certification exam preparation
- Portfolio development
- Continuing education opportunities
- Professional development
- Industry trends

## Required Certifications

### OSHA 10-Hour General Industry
**Topics:**
- Walking and working surfaces
- Exit routes and emergency action plans
- Electrical safety
- Personal protective equipment
- Hazard communication
- Materials handling
- Machine guarding
- Bloodborne pathogens (if applicable)

### EPA 608 Type I Certification
**For:** Small appliance refrigerant handling  
**Topics:**
- Core knowledge
- Type I specific knowledge
- Refrigerant recovery procedures
- Environmental regulations

### Optional Certifications
- Certified Apartment Maintenance Technician (CAMT)
- Certified Pool Operator (CPO)
- Backflow Prevention Certification
- Forklift Operator Certification
- First Aid/CPR

## Daily Responsibilities

### Typical Tasks
- Respond to maintenance requests
- Perform preventive maintenance
- Inspect facilities and equipment
- Make repairs (plumbing, electrical, carpentry)
- Maintain grounds and common areas
- Coordinate with vendors and contractors
- Document work completed
- Order supplies and parts
- Ensure safety compliance
- Respond to emergencies

### Work Schedule
- Typically Monday-Friday, 8am-5pm
- On-call rotation for emergencies
- Some weekend work may be required
- Overtime during busy seasons
- Emergency response 24/7 (varies by employer)
