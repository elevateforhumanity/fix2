## What is Workforce Development?

Workforce development refers to programs and initiatives that help individuals gain the skills, credentials, and experience needed for employment in high-demand occupations.

### Key Components:

**Skills Training:**

- Technical skills for specific occupations
- Soft skills (communication, teamwork, problem-solving)
- Digital literacy
- Industry-recognized certifications

**Career Pathways:**

- Entry-level positions
- Advancement opportunities
- Stackable credentials
- Lifelong learning

**Support Services:**

- Career counseling
- Job placement assistance
- Childcare and transportation support
- Financial aid and funding

## Funding Sources

### 1. WIOA (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act)

**What It Covers:**

- Full tuition for approved training programs
- Books and supplies
- Transportation assistance
- Childcare support
- Testing fees

**Eligibility:**

- 18+ years old (16-17 with special circumstances)
- U.S. citizen or authorized to work
- Registered with Selective Service (if applicable)
- Meet income requirements (if applicable)
- Require training to obtain/retain employment

**Priority Groups:**

1. Veterans and eligible spouses
2. Recipients of public assistance
3. Low-income individuals
4. Basic skills deficient individuals

**How to Apply:**

1. Visit local WorkOne Career Center
2. Meet with career advisor
3. Complete assessment
4. Develop Individual Employment Plan (IEP)
5. Get approval (typically 1-2 weeks)
6. Enroll in training

**WorkOne Locations:**

- Indianapolis: 3 locations
- Fort Wayne: 2 locations
- Evansville: 1 location
- South Bend: 1 location
- 20+ additional locations statewide

### 2. Workforce Ready Grant (WRG)

**What It Covers:**

- Up to $7,500 per year
- Tuition and fees only
- Renewable for up to 2 years

**Eligibility:**

- Indiana resident
- U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen
- High school diploma or equivalent
- Not owe refund on state/federal grant
- Not in default on student loan
- Enrolled in eligible program

**Eligible Programs:**

- Healthcare certifications
- Manufacturing credentials
- Information technology
- Transportation and logistics
- Building and construction
- 200+ approved programs

**How to Apply:**

1. Complete FAFSA
2. Apply through state portal
3. Enroll in eligible program
4. Maintain satisfactory progress

### 3. Justice Reinvestment Initiative (JRI)

**What It Covers:**

- Full training costs
- Support services
- Job placement assistance
- Mentorship

**Eligibility:**

- Currently or formerly incarcerated
- Referred by probation/parole officer
- Committed to program completion
- Meet program requirements

**Focus Areas:**

- Skilled trades
- Manufacturing
- Healthcare
- Transportation

### 4. Employer Sponsorship

**How It Works:**

- Employer pays for training
- Commitment to work for employer
- Often includes paid training time
- May include benefits

**Industries Offering:**

- Healthcare (hospitals, nursing homes)
- Manufacturing (major employers)
- Transportation (trucking companies)
- Construction (contractors)

### 5. Trade Adjustment Assistance (TAA)

**What It Covers:**

- Training costs
- Income support during training
- Job search allowances
- Relocation allowances

**Eligibility:**

- Lost job due to foreign trade
- Company certified by Department of Labor
- Meet other TAA requirements

## High-Demand Careers in Indiana

### Healthcare (15,000+ openings)

**Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA):**

- Training: 4-6 weeks
- Cost: Free with WIOA/WRG
- Starting Pay: $14-$18/hour
- Growth: 8% annually

**Medical Assistant:**

- Training: 12 weeks
- Cost: Free with WIOA/WRG
- Starting Pay: $16-$22/hour
- Growth: 16% annually

**Phlebotomy Technician:**

- Training: 8 weeks
- Cost: Free with WIOA/WRG
- Starting Pay: $15-$20/hour
- Growth: 17% annually

### Skilled Trades (10,000+ openings)

**HVAC Technician:**

- Training: 6-9 months
- Cost: Free with WIOA
- Starting Pay: $18-$25/hour
- Experienced: $30-$40/hour
- Growth: 15% annually

**Electrician:**

- Training: Apprenticeship (4 years)
- Cost: Earn while you learn
- Starting Pay: $18-$22/hour
- Journeyman: $30-$45/hour
- Growth: 9% annually

**Plumber:**

- Training: Apprenticeship (4-5 years)
- Cost: Earn while you learn
- Starting Pay: $16-$20/hour
- Journeyman: $28-$42/hour
- Growth: 5% annually

### Transportation (8,000+ openings)

**CDL Driver:**

- Training: 4-6 weeks
- Cost: Free with WIOA or employer-paid
- Starting Pay: $50,000-$65,000/year
- Experienced: $70,000-$90,000/year
- Sign-on bonuses: $5,000-$10,000

**Forklift Operator:**

- Training: 2 days
- Cost: $200-$400 (often employer-paid)
- Starting Pay: $16-$20/hour
- Growth: Steady demand

### Manufacturing (12,000+ openings)

- Experienced: $28-$35/hour
- Growth: 7% annually

**Welder:**

- Training: 6-12 months
- Cost: Free with WIOA
- Starting Pay: $18-$25/hour

- Growth: 3% annually

### Personal Services (5,000+ openings)

- Cost: Free with WIOA

## How to Get Started

### Step 1: Self-Assessment

**Questions to Ask:**

- What are my interests and strengths?
- What is my current skill level?
- How much time can I commit to training?
- What are my financial needs during training?
- What are my long-term career goals?

**Tools:**

- O\*NET Interest Profiler
- WorkOne Career Assessments
- Skills Gap Analysis

### Step 2: Research Careers

**Resources:**

- Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)
- Indiana Department of Workforce Development
- O\*NET Online
- Industry associations
- Informational interviews

**Key Factors:**

- Job outlook and growth
- Salary and benefits
- Training requirements
- Work environment
- Advancement opportunities

### Step 3: Explore Funding

**Priority Order:**

1. WIOA (most comprehensive)
2. Workforce Ready Grant (Indiana residents)
3. Employer sponsorship (no debt)
4. JRI (if eligible)
5. TAA (if eligible)

**Application Timeline:**

- WIOA: 2-4 weeks
- WRG: 4-6 weeks
- Employer: Varies
- JRI: 1-2 weeks

### Step 4: Choose Training Provider

**Accreditation:**

- State-approved
- Industry-recognized
- Employer-endorsed

**Quality Indicators:**

- Completion rates
- Job placement rates
- Graduate satisfaction
- Employer feedback

**Program Features:**

- Hands-on training
- Industry certifications
- Job placement assistance
- Flexible scheduling

### Step 5: Apply and Enroll

**Application Checklist:**

- Government-issued ID
- Social Security card
- High school diploma/GED
- Proof of residency
- Income documentation (if applicable)
- Selective Service registration (males 18-25)

**Enrollment Process:**

1. Submit application
2. Meet with advisor
3. Complete assessment
4. Secure funding
5. Enroll in program
6. Begin training

### Step 6: Complete Training

**Success Strategies:**

- Attend all classes
- Complete assignments on time
- Ask questions
- Build relationships with instructors
- Network with classmates
- Participate in job placement services

### Step 7: Launch Career

**Job Search:**

- Resume and cover letter
- Online job boards
- Networking
- Career fairs
- Direct employer contact
- WorkOne job matching

**Interview Preparation:**

- Research employers
- Practice common questions
- Prepare questions to ask
- Professional attire
- Follow-up
