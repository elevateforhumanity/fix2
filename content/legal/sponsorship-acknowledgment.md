# Sponsorship, Curriculum & Onboarding Overview

**Required Partner Acknowledgment**

---

## Elevate for Humanity's Role as Program Sponsor

Elevate for Humanity serves as the official **Program Sponsor** for workforce, apprenticeship, and funded training programs listed under our umbrella.

As the Sponsor, Elevate for Humanity is responsible for:

- Program approval and sponsorship
- Workforce system alignment (WIOA, WRG, JRI, apprenticeship, etc.)
- Compliance and reporting
- Enrollment processing and eligibility tracking
- Curriculum verification and completion tracking
- Payment coordination and disbursement

**All programs operate under Elevate for Humanity sponsorship, even when partners deliver instruction or hands-on training.**

---

## Partner Curriculum & Program Naming

Partners may:

- Use their own curriculum, instructional methods, or hands-on training model
- Operate under their own program name for branding and marketing purposes

However, the following requirements apply:

- Students must complete the required online curriculum components designated by Elevate for Humanity (for compliance and credentialing)
- Elevate for Humanity will be listed as the **Program Sponsor** on:
  - Enrollment records
  - Workforce documentation
  - Funding submissions
  - Completion and reporting documents

**Example:**

> "XYZ Nail Technician Program — Sponsored by Elevate for Humanity"

This structure allows flexibility while maintaining funding and regulatory compliance.

---

## Student Onboarding & Enrollment Process

### How Students Enter the Program

1. Student submits an inquiry or application through Elevate for Humanity
2. Student is referred to WorkOne (or appropriate agency) when funding is involved
3. Eligibility is reviewed and documented
4. Student is officially enrolled under the sponsored program
5. Access credentials are issued (online curriculum + partner training site)

**Partners do not independently enroll students into funded programs.**

All enrollments must flow through Elevate for Humanity to ensure:

- Funding eligibility
- Proper documentation
- Accurate reporting

---

## WorkOne & Funding Coordination (What Partners Need to Know)

WorkOne and workforce agencies:

- Determine funding eligibility
- Approve training plans
- Require sponsor-level reporting

Elevate for Humanity manages:

- Communication with WorkOne
- Submission of program and student data
- Reporting outcomes and completions

Partners may be asked to:

- Verify attendance or hours
- Confirm hands-on training milestones
- Provide progress updates

**Partners do not negotiate funding directly with WorkOne unless authorized in writing.**

---

## Partner Responsibilities During Training

Partners agree to:

- Provide instruction and/or hands-on training as outlined
- Track and submit attendance or hours on schedule
- Notify Elevate for Humanity of disengagement or issues
- Cooperate with audits or verification requests

Partners are not responsible for:

- Workforce funding approvals
- State or federal reporting submissions
- Student eligibility determinations
