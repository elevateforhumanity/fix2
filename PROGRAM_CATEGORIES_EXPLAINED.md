## üéØ Two Types of Programs

### 1. MICRO PROGRAMS (50% Split After 100% Markup)
**What They Are**: Pre-built courses from credentialed partners' catalogs - SOLD DIRECTLY BY ELEVATE on the website

**Revenue Share**: 50% to Elevate, 50% to partner (after 100% markup on partner's base cost)

**Source**: Partner catalogs and course libraries

**Who Sells Them**: ELEVATE ONLY - These appear on the website for students to purchase directly

**How It Works**:
- Partner base cost: $500
- 100% markup: $500
- Total price to student: $1,000
- **Elevate gets: $500 (50%)**
- **Partner gets: $500 (50%)**

**Example**:
- Partner charges: $500 for Phlebotomy course
- Elevate adds 100% markup: +$500
- Student pays: $1,000
- Split: $500 to Elevate, $500 to partner

**Examples**:
- Phlebotomy Technician (from partner catalog)
- EKG Technician (from partner catalog)
- Pharmacy Technician (from partner catalog)
- Dental Assistant (from partner catalog)
- Patient Care Technician (from partner catalog)
- Sterile Processing (from partner catalog)
- Medical Billing & Coding (from partner catalog)
- Any other short courses from credentialed partner libraries

**How It Works**:
1. Partner provides the course content
2. Partner charges base fee (e.g., $500)
3. We add 50% markup ($500 ‚Üí $1,000)
4. You get $250, Elevate gets $250, Partner gets $500

**Your Role**:
- Facilitate the course
- Monitor student progress
- Track attendance
- Provide support
- Submit documentation

**You DON'T**:
- Create curriculum
- Develop content
- Build assessments
- Own intellectual property

---

### 2. PROGRAM HOLDER PROGRAMS (1/3 Split)
**What They Are**: WIOA, WRG, and Apprenticeship programs delivered by program holders

**Revenue Share**: 1/3 to program holder, 2/3 to Elevate (after partner fees, books, tests, supplies)

**Source**: Created and delivered by program holders (following master syllabus)

**Program Types**:
- **WIOA Programs** (grant-funded workforce training)
- **WRG Programs** (Workforce Ready Grants)
- **Apprenticeship Programs** (registered apprenticeships with hour tracking)

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ CPR Certification
- ‚úÖ Financial Literacy
- ‚úÖ Peer Recovery Coach
- ‚úÖ Tax Office Certification
- Medical Assistant
- CNA Training
- HVAC Technician
- Welding
- Electrical
- Plumbing
- CDL Training
- And other ETPL-approved programs

**How It Works**:
1. You create curriculum (following master syllabus)
2. You deliver instruction
3. You assess students
4. You issue certificates
5. Program is on Indiana ETPL list

**Your Role**:
- Develop curriculum (within master syllabus framework)
- Deliver instruction
- Create assessments
- Track progress
- Manage students
- Issue credentials

**You DO**:
- Own your teaching methods
- Create your materials
- Design your approach
- Build your reputation

---

### 3. APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAMS (1/3 Split)
**What They Are**: Registered apprenticeship programs combining classroom + on-the-job training

**Revenue Share**: 1/3 to program holder, 2/3 to Elevate (after partner fees, books, tests, supplies)

**Source**: Created and delivered by YOU with employer partnerships

**Examples**:
- ‚úÖ Barber Apprenticeship (1,500 hours)
- ‚úÖ Esthetician Apprenticeship (600 hours)
- ‚úÖ Culinary Arts Apprenticeship
- HVAC Apprenticeship
- Electrical Apprenticeship
- Plumbing Apprenticeship
- Carpentry Apprenticeship
- And other registered apprenticeships

**How It Works**:
1. You provide classroom instruction
2. Employer provides on-the-job training
3. Student tracks hours (clock in/out)
4. Student earns while learning
5. Leads to state licensure

**Your Role**:
- Classroom instruction
- Curriculum development
- Hour verification
- Employer coordination
- Licensure preparation

**Special Features**:
- Hour tracking system
- Employer partnerships
- Earn while you learn
- State licensure pathway
- Longer duration (6-24 months)

## ‚ùì Common Questions

**Q: Can I offer all three types?**
A: Yes! You can offer micro programs from partners, your own ETPL programs, and apprenticeships.

**Q: Why is micro program split 50/50 but others 1/3?**
A: Micro programs are pre-built by partners. You're facilitating, not creating. ETPL and apprenticeships require you to develop and deliver instruction.

**Q: Can I customize micro programs?**
A: No. Micro programs are the partner's intellectual property. You facilitate their content as-is.

**Q: Can I import my LMS content for micro programs?**
A: No for micro programs (partner's content). Yes for ETPL and apprenticeship programs (your content).

**Q: Do all programs use the master syllabus?**
A: No. Only ETPL and apprenticeship programs use master syllabus. Micro programs use partner's curriculum.
