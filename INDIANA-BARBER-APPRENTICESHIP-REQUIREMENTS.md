# Indiana Barber Apprenticeship Program - Official Requirements

**Program Type**: DOL Registered Apprenticeship (House Bill 1135)  
**Partner**: Milady CIMA for Theory Instruction  
**Status**: State-Approved Alternative to Traditional Barbering School

---

## Program Overview

### What is the Indiana Barber Apprenticeship?

Indiana's barber apprenticeship program, established by **House Bill 1135**, allows aspiring barbers to become licensed **without attending traditional barbering school** by earning on-the-job training and a wage.

**Key Benefits:**
- ✅ **Earn While You Learn** - Get paid while training
- ✅ **No Tuition Costs** - Save thousands compared to traditional school
- ✅ **Hands-On Experience** - Work directly with experienced barbers
- ✅ **State-Recognized** - Leads to Indiana Barber License
- ✅ **DOL Registered** - Federally recognized apprenticeship

---

## Program Structure

### Total Training Hours: 2,000 Hours

**Breakdown:**
- **Theory Instruction**: 400 hours (via Milady CIMA online)
- **Practical Training**: 1,600 hours (on-the-job at barbershop)

### Timeline: Approximately 12-18 months

### Apprentice Requirements:

**Age & Education:**
- ✅ Minimum age: **16 years old** with 10th grade completion

## Program Holder (Employer) Responsibilities

### Who Can Be a Program Holder?

**Wilders Barbershop** or any licensed barbershop in Indiana can sponsor apprentices if they meet these requirements:

### Required Qualifications:

1. **Licensed Master Barber** on staff with minimum 3 years experience
2. **Active Indiana Barber Shop License** in good standing
3. **Registered with Indiana State Board of Barbers**
4. **DOL Apprenticeship Sponsor** registration (Elevate assists with this)
5. **Safe, compliant work environment** meeting state health codes

### Program Holder Responsibilities:

#### 1. Training & Supervision
- ✅ Provide **1,600 hours of supervised on-the-job training**
- ✅ Assign a **qualified mentor barber** (licensed, 3+ years experience)
- ✅ Ensure apprentice works on diverse hair types and services
- ✅ Teach all required barbering skills per Indiana standards
- ✅ Document training hours weekly using approved timesheets

#### 2. Compensation
- ✅ Pay apprentice **progressive wages** (starts at 50% of journeyman wage, increases to 90%)
- ✅ Provide regular paychecks (W-2 employee, not 1099)
- ✅ Follow all labor laws (minimum wage, overtime, etc.)
- ✅ Optional: Provide benefits (health insurance, paid time off)

#### 3. Documentation & Reporting
- ✅ Submit **monthly progress reports** to Elevate for Humanity
- ✅ Track apprentice hours in **RAPIDS system** (DOL database)
- ✅ Complete **quarterly evaluations** of apprentice performance
- ✅ Maintain records for **minimum 5 years** after completion
- ✅ Report any issues or concerns immediately

#### 4. Compliance
- ✅ Follow **Indiana State Board of Barbers** regulations
- ✅ Maintain **active shop license** throughout apprenticeship
- ✅ Allow **state inspections** of training environment
- ✅ Ensure apprentice completes **Milady theory hours**
- ✅ Prepare apprentice for **state board examination**

#### 5. Partnership with Elevate for Humanity
- ✅ Attend **quarterly program holder meetings**
- ✅ Communicate regularly with Elevate program coordinator
- ✅ Allow Elevate staff to conduct **site visits**
- ✅ Participate in **apprentice evaluations**
- ✅ Provide feedback on program improvements

## Curriculum Requirements

### Theory Instruction (400 Hours via Milady CIMA)

**Delivered Online Through Milady CIMA Platform:**

#### Module 1: Barbering Basics (80 hours)
- History of barbering
- Professional image and conduct
- Infection control and safety
- Tools and equipment
- Shop management basics

#### Module 2: Sciences (80 hours)
- Anatomy and physiology
- Skin structure and disorders
- Hair structure and growth
- Chemistry for barbers
- Bacteriology and sanitation

#### Module 3: Hair Services (120 hours)
- Haircutting techniques
- Clipper work and fading
- Scissor techniques
- Razor work
- Hair styling and finishing

#### Module 4: Shaving & Facial Services (60 hours)
- Beard trimming and design
- Straight razor shaving
- Facial treatments
- Mustache and sideburn design

#### Module 5: Business & Professional Development (60 hours)
- Customer service excellence
- Building clientele
- Retail sales
- Shop operations
- State laws and regulations

**Assessment:**
- ✅ Milady online quizzes and exams
- ✅ Minimum 75% passing score on all modules
- ✅ Final comprehensive exam
- ✅ Certificate of completion from Milady

## Practical Training (1,600 Hours On-the-Job)

### Required Services & Minimum Repetitions:

**Haircutting:**
- Basic haircuts: 125 services
- Clipper cuts: 100 services
- Fades and tapers: 100 services
- Scissor cuts: 75 services
- Children's cuts: 30 services

**Shaving & Facial:**
- Beard trims: 60 services
- Straight razor shaves: 30 services
- Facial treatments: 15 services
- Mustache/sideburn design: 30 services

**Styling:**
- Blow-dry styling: 50 services
- Product application: 60 services
- Special occasion styling: 15 services

**Business Operations:**
- Client consultations: 125 interactions
- Retail sales: 30 transactions
- Appointment scheduling: 60 bookings
- Shop maintenance: Ongoing

**Documentation:**
- ✅ Apprentice maintains **service log book**
- ✅ Mentor signs off on each service category
- ✅ Monthly review with program holder
- ✅ Quarterly evaluation by Elevate coordinator

## Wage Progression Schedule

### Progressive Wage Structure:

**Year 1 (Months 1-6):**
- Starting wage: **50% of journeyman barber wage**
- Example: If journeyman earns $20/hour, apprentice earns $10/hour
- Plus tips (if shop allows)

**Year 1 (Months 7-12):**
- Wage increase: **60% of journeyman wage**
- Example: $12/hour if journeyman earns $20/hour

**Year 2 (Months 13-18):**
- Wage increase: **75% of journeyman wage**
- Example: $15/hour if journeyman earns $20/hour

**Year 2 (Months 19-24):**
- Wage increase: **90% of journeyman wage**
- Example: $18/hour if journeyman earns $20/hour

**Upon Licensure:**
- Full journeyman wage: **100%**
- Example: $20/hour plus tips

## State Board Examination

### After Completing 2,000 Hours:

**Eligibility:**
- ✅ Completed 400 hours Milady theory (verified certificate)
- ✅ Completed 1,600 hours practical training (verified by program holder)
- ✅ Submitted all required documentation to Indiana State Board
- ✅ Paid examination fee ($100-150)

**Examination Components:**

**Written Exam:**
- 100 multiple choice questions
- Covers theory, sanitation, laws, and regulations
- Minimum passing score: **75%**
- 2 hours to complete

**Practical Exam:**
- Demonstrate haircutting skills
- Demonstrate shaving techniques
- Demonstrate sanitation procedures
- Scored by licensed examiners
- Minimum passing score: **75%**

**Upon Passing:**
- ✅ Receive **Indiana Barber License**
- ✅ License valid for 2 years
- ✅ Renewable with continuing education
- ✅ Can work as licensed barber anywhere in Indiana

## Frequently Asked Questions

### For Apprentices:

**Q: Can I work at any barbershop?**
A: You must work at a registered apprenticeship sponsor shop. Elevate helps match you with participating shops.

**Q: What if I need to change shops?**
A: Contact Elevate immediately. We can help facilitate a transfer if needed, and your hours will transfer.

**Q: Can I work part-time?**
A: Yes, but it will take longer to complete 3,200 hours. Full-time (40 hours/week) takes about 18-24 months.

**Q: What if I fail the state board exam?**
A: You can retake it. Elevate provides additional exam prep support.

**Q: Can I get financial aid?**
A: Yes! WIOA funding covers Milady costs. You may also qualify for supportive services (transportation, childcare, etc.).

### For Program Holders:

**Q: How many apprentices can I have?**
A: Ratio is typically 1 apprentice per 2 licensed barbers. Elevate will help determine your capacity.

**Q: What if the apprentice doesn't work out?**
A: Follow termination procedures in agreement. Elevate will work with you to resolve issues or find a replacement.

**Q: Am I required to hire them after completion?**
A: No, but most shops do. You've invested in their training and they know your business.

**Q: What are my legal obligations?**
A: Treat apprentice as W-2 employee. Follow all labor laws. Maintain safe work environment. Document training.

**Q: How much time does mentoring require?**
A: Daily supervision during work hours. Monthly paperwork (1-2 hours). Quarterly meetings with Elevate.
