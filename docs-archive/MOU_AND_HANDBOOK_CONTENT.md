#### 1. PURPOSE OF THIS MOU

Establishes partnership framework for delivering workforce training programs (WRG, WIOA, JRI, EmployIndy, DOL-registered apprenticeships) and revenue sharing structure.

#### 2. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

**2.1 Elevate for Humanity will:**

- Serve as primary training sponsor and system of record
- Provide and maintain LMS, participant portal, case notes, certificates, reporting tools
- Coordinate with state/local workforce agencies, credentialing partners, funding sources
- Enroll participants and track participation, hours, completions, certifications
- Manage collection of funds from workforce programs
- Calculate and issue revenue share payments

**2.2 Program Holder will:**

- Provide safe, professional training environment (physical space, supervision)
- Deliver hands-on training, coaching, supervision per curriculum requirements
- Maintain attendance expectations, sign-in procedures, participant communication
- Enter case notes, progress updates, follow-up info into Elevate portal
- Notify Elevate of issues impacting participation, safety, eligibility
- Comply with workplace safety, nondiscrimination, confidentiality, workforce program laws

#### 3. PROGRAMS COVERED

- Barber / Beauty Apprenticeship
- CNA / Patient Care / Home Health
- HVAC / Building Technician / Construction
- Peer Support / Recovery Coach
- Financial Literacy / Tax Training
- Other industry-recognized credential programs

Programs can be added/updated via email or signed addendum.

#### 4. COMPENSATION AND REVENUE SHARE

**4.1 Definition of Net Program Revenue**

```
Net Program Revenue =
  Total eligible training revenue received by Elevate
  MINUS direct required program costs:
    - Credentialing partner fees (testing, licensing, exams)
    - Required background checks, drug screens, clearances
    - Approved learner toolkits and equipment (kits, books, scrubs, PPE, tools)
    - Platform, processing, compliance fees
```

**4.2 Program Holder Share (Configurable % Model)**

- Program Holder receives X% of Net Program Revenue per participant
- Paid after enrollment verified, costs accounted for, funds received
- Elevate retains remaining revenue for admin, platform, staffing, compliance

**4.3 Timing of Payments**

- Monthly or quarterly cycle after funds received and reconciled
- Summary statement provided showing:
  - Number of participants
  - Funding source(s)
  - Gross revenue, expenses, Net Program Revenue
  - Amount payable to Program Holder

**4.4 Non-Funded / Free Participants**

- Free, pilot, scholarship, internally funded participants may be included/excluded
- Exceptions clarified in writing

#### 5. DATA, REPORTING, AND CASE NOTES

- Program Holder gets secure delegate portal access
- Must enter case notes, statuses (On Track, At Risk, Not Engaged), follow-up dates
- Elevate maintains training records, certificates, participation reports
- Reports shared with agencies, funders, employers per program rules and privacy laws

#### 6. TERM AND TERMINATION

- Effective on date of last signature
- Either party may terminate with 30 days' written notice
- Elevate may suspend/terminate immediately for serious non-compliance, safety concerns, fraud
- Upon termination, Elevate pays earned amounts for properly enrolled participants

#### 7. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR RELATIONSHIP

- Program Holder is independent contractor
- Not employment, partnership, or joint venture
- Program Holder responsible for own employees, taxes, insurance, operations

#### 8. NON-EXCLUSIVITY

- Non-exclusive agreement
- Elevate may partner with other Program Holders
- Program Holder may work with other training sponsors
- Must not violate program rules or confidentiality

#### 9. CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT (NDA)

**9.1 Confidential Information includes:**

- Participant personal information (names, SSN, employment history, criminal background, health info, PII)
- Training curricula, materials, assessments, proprietary content
- Business strategies, financial info, pricing, revenue data
- Funding source relationships, grant applications, compliance docs
- Technology systems, software, databases, IP
- Trade secrets, business methods, operational procedures

**9.2 Obligations:**

- Maintain strict confidentiality
- Use only for MOU purposes
- No third-party disclosure without written consent
- Implement reasonable security measures
- Limit access to need-to-know basis
- Return or destroy upon termination

**9.3 Exceptions:**

- Publicly available information
- Rightfully possessed prior to disclosure
- Independently developed
- Required by law/court order (with prior notice when permissible)

**9.4 Data Protection and FERPA/HIPAA Compliance:**

- Comply with FERPA, HIPAA, all privacy regulations
- Implement administrative, physical, technical safeguards
- Report data breaches immediately
- Cooperate with investigations

**9.5 Duration:**

- 5 years from disclosure date
- Indefinite for trade secrets and participant PII

#### 10. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

**10.1 Non-Compete Agreement (2 years post-termination):**
Program Holder agrees NOT to:

- Compete with Elevate in same geographic area and target population
- Use Elevate's proprietary materials to establish competing program
- Solicit funding from Elevate's sources without written consent

**10.2 Non-Solicitation of Participants (2 years):**

- No direct solicitation of Elevate participants
- No encouraging participants to leave Elevate programs
- No using participant contact info for other purposes

**10.3 Non-Solicitation of Staff and Partners (1 year):**

- No soliciting/hiring Elevate employees, contractors, consultants
- No interfering with Elevate's partner relationships
- No inducing partners/vendors to terminate with Elevate

**10.4 Reasonable Scope:**
Restrictions protect:

- Confidential and proprietary information
- Funding source relationships
- Program integrity and participant continuity
- Investments in training infrastructure

**10.5 Exceptions:**

- Operating existing non-competing business
- Accepting participants who independently seek services
- Providing training in areas not served by Elevate
- Partnering on new programs with mutual agreement

**10.6 Remedies for Breach:**

- Immediate injunctive relief
- Recovery of all damages (lost revenue, funding)
- Attorney's fees and costs
- Other legal/equitable remedies

#### 11. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**11.1 Elevate Property:**

- All training materials, curricula, assessments, software, branding remain Elevate's property
- Program Holder gets limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable license

**11.2 Program Holder Property:**

- Pre-existing IP remains Program Holder's property
- Modifications/derivatives using Elevate materials jointly owned or licensed back

**11.3 Participant Work Product:**

- Owned by participants
- Elevate retains right to use for promotional/educational/reporting with attribution

#### 12. ENTIRE UNDERSTANDING AND AMENDMENTS

- Represents complete understanding
- May be updated/amended in writing (including email)
- Requires agreement from both parties
