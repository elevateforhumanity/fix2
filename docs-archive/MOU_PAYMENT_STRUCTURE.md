# MOU Payment Structure - Updated

**Last Updated:** January 1, 2026  
**Status:** ✅ Universal 1/3 Model with Two-Payment Structure

---

## Key Changes Made

### 1. Universal Payment Rate

**ALL Program Holders receive the same rate:**

- **1/3 (33.33%)** of Net Program Revenue per participant
- Ensures fairness and consistency across the network
- No negotiation needed - standard for all partners

### 2. Two-Payment Structure

#### Payment 1: Mid-Enrollment (16.67% of Net Revenue)

**Trigger:** Participant reaches 50% completion of training hours

**Requirements:**

- Enrollment verified
- Mid-point progress documented
- Participant actively engaged

**Timing:** Processed within 30 days of milestone

**Amount:** 50% of Program Holder Share (16.67% of Net Program Revenue)

#### Payment 2: Completion (16.67% of Net Revenue)

**Trigger:** Participant completes training and earns credential

**Requirements:**

- Training completed
- Credential issued
- Final verification complete

**Timing:** Processed within 30 days of credential issuance

**Amount:** 50% of Program Holder Share (16.67% of Net Program Revenue)

### 3. Month-to-Month Agreement

**NEW:** Agreement operates on month-to-month basis

**Benefits:**

- No long-term commitment required
- Flexibility for both parties
- Easy to start partnership
- Easy to end if not working out

**Termination:**

- Either party can terminate with 30 days' written notice
- Notice via email
- Immediate termination for serious violations only

### 4. Wraparound Services Funding

**Elevate retains 2/3 (66.67%) to fund:**

**Administrative Costs:**

- Platform maintenance and development
- Staffing and coordination
- Compliance and reporting
- Quality assurance

**Wraparound Services for Participants:**

- Case management and support services
- Career counseling and job placement
- Transportation assistance
- Childcare assistance
- Emergency financial assistance
- Mental health and substance abuse support
- Technology and equipment loans
- Tutoring and academic support

## Payment Example

**Scenario:** Student enrolled in Barber Apprenticeship

**Funding Received:** $6,000 from WIOA

**Direct Program Costs:**

- State Board exam fee: $150
- Barber kit and supplies: $350
- Background check: $50
- **Total Direct Costs:** $550

**Net Program Revenue:** $6,000 - $550 = $5,450

**Program Holder Share (1/3):** $5,450 × 0.3333 = $1,816.50

**Payment Breakdown:**

- **Payment 1 (Mid-Enrollment):** $908.25 (when student reaches 600 hours)
- **Payment 2 (Completion):** $908.25 (when student completes 1,200 hours and passes exam)

**Elevate Retains:** $3,633.50 for admin, platform, and wraparound services

## Payment Timeline

### Month 1-3: Enrollment Phase

- Student enrolls
- Orientation and onboarding
- Begin training
- No payment yet

### Month 4-6: Mid-Enrollment

- Student reaches 50% completion (e.g., 600 of 1,200 hours)
- Progress documented
- **Payment 1 processed** (16.67% of Net Revenue)
- Within 30 days of milestone

### Month 7-12: Completion Phase

- Student completes remaining hours
- Takes and passes credential exam
- Credential issued
- **Payment 2 processed** (16.67% of Net Revenue)
- Within 30 days of credential issuance

## Termination Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Program Holder Terminates

**Notice:** 30 days written notice

**Payments:**

- Payment 1: Paid for students who reached 50% before termination
- Payment 2: Paid for students who completed before termination
- No payment for students who didn't reach milestones

**Transition:** Cooperate to transfer active students

### Scenario 2: Elevate Terminates (With Notice)

**Notice:** 30 days written notice

**Payments:** Same as Scenario 1

**Reasons:** Performance issues, quality concerns, business decision

### Scenario 3: Elevate Terminates (Immediate)

**Notice:** None - immediate termination

**Reasons:**

- Serious non-compliance (WIOA, FERPA violations)
- Safety violations
- Fraud or falsification
- Breach of confidentiality
- Misuse of funds

**Payments:** Only for milestones reached before termination date

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What if a student drops out before 50% completion?

**A:** No payment to Program Holder. Elevate absorbs the loss.

### Q: What if a student completes training but fails the credential exam?

**A:** Payment 1 already paid. Payment 2 held until student passes exam (if they retake it).

### Q: Can the 1/3 rate be negotiated?

**A:** No. Universal rate ensures fairness for all Program Holders.

### Q: How long does it take to get paid after milestone?

**A:** Within 30 days of milestone verification.

### Q: What if funding is delayed from the agency?

**A:** Payments processed after Elevate receives and clears funds. May cause delay.

### Q: Can I terminate if I'm not happy?

**A:** Yes. 30 days' notice, no penalty. Month-to-month agreement.

### Q: What happens to students if agreement terminates?

**A:** Elevate works with you to transition students to alternative arrangements or complete training.
