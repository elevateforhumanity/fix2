**Elevate for Humanity Career & Training Institute**

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Last Reviewed:** December 12, 2025  
**Next Review:** December 12, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This manual establishes policies and procedures for compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), 20 U.S.C. ยง 1232g, protecting the privacy of student education records.

---

## 2. Scope

These policies apply to all education records maintained by Elevate for Humanity Career & Training Institute, including electronic records in the Learning Management System (LMS).

---

## 3. Definitions

### Education Records
Any record directly related to a student maintained by the institution, including:
- Academic transcripts and grades
- Attendance records
- Enrollment information
- Financial aid records
- Disciplinary records
- LMS activity and submissions
- Assessment results
- Correspondence regarding the student

### Directory Information
Information that may be disclosed without consent:
- Student name
- Program of study
- Enrollment dates
- Degrees and awards received
- Participation in activities

Students may opt out of directory information disclosure.

### Legitimate Educational Interest
School officials need access to education records to:
- Perform administrative tasks
- Provide educational services
- Maintain safety and security
- Comply with legal obligations

---

## 4. Student Rights Under FERPA

Students have the right to:

### 4.1 Inspect and Review Records
- Request access to education records within 45 days
- Receive copies of records (fees may apply)
- Request explanations of record contents

### 4.2 Request Amendment
- Request correction of inaccurate or misleading records
- Request formal hearing if amendment denied
- Place statement in record if disagreement persists

### 4.3 Consent to Disclosure
- Control disclosure of personally identifiable information
- Provide written consent for third-party access
- Revoke consent at any time

### 4.4 File Complaints
- File complaints with the U.S. Department of Education
- Report violations to institutional FERPA officer
- Request investigation of unauthorized disclosures

---

## 5. Disclosure Without Consent

Records may be disclosed without consent to:

### 5.1 School Officials
- Employees with legitimate educational interest
- Contractors performing institutional services
- Officials conducting audits or evaluations

### 5.2 Other Educational Institutions
- Schools where student seeks enrollment
- Schools where student is enrolled

### 5.3 Authorized Representatives
- Federal, state, or local education authorities
- Organizations conducting studies for the institution
- Accrediting organizations

### 5.4 Financial Aid Purposes
- Determining eligibility
- Administering aid programs
- Enforcing terms and conditions

### 5.5 Legal Requirements
- Compliance with judicial orders or subpoenas
- Health and safety emergencies
- Law enforcement purposes (limited circumstances)

### 5.6 Directory Information
- If student has not opted out
- For legitimate purposes only

---

## 6. LMS-Specific FERPA Protections

### 6.1 Access Controls
- Role-based permissions (student, instructor, admin)
- Students access only their own records
- Instructors access only assigned students
- Administrators access based on job function

### 6.2 Authentication
- Unique user credentials required
- Strong password requirements enforced
- Multi-factor authentication recommended
- Automatic session timeouts

### 6.3 Data Security
- Encrypted data transmission (SSL/TLS)
- Encrypted data storage
- Regular security audits
- Backup and disaster recovery

### 6.4 Audit Logging
- All record access logged
- Login attempts tracked
- Changes to records documented
- Reports available for review

---

## 7. Record Retention and Disposal

### 7.1 Retention Periods
- Academic records: Permanent
- Attendance records: 5 years after completion
- Financial records: 7 years
- Disciplinary records: 7 years
- LMS activity logs: 3 years
- Application materials: 3 years (if not enrolled)

### 7.2 Secure Disposal
- Electronic records: Secure deletion/overwriting
- Physical records: Shredding or incineration
- Disposal documented in destruction log
- Third-party disposal services must sign confidentiality agreements

---

## 8. Third-Party Access

### 8.1 Data Sharing Agreements
Required for:
- Workforce development partners
- External LMS providers
- Assessment vendors
- Background check services

Agreements must specify:
- Purpose of disclosure
- Data elements shared
- Security requirements
- Prohibition on re-disclosure
- Data retention and destruction

### 8.2 Student Consent
- Written consent required for non-FERPA exceptions
- Consent must specify records, purpose, and recipient
- Consent valid for specific timeframe
- Student may revoke consent

---

## 9. Directory Information Opt-Out

### 9.1 Notification
Students notified of directory information policy:
- During orientation
- In student handbook
- On institutional website
- Annually via email

### 9.2 Opt-Out Process
- Written request to Registrar
- Effective within 10 business days
- Remains in effect until revoked
- Student may opt back in at any time

---

## 10. FERPA Training Requirements

### 10.1 Mandatory Training
All employees with access to education records must:
- Complete FERPA training within 30 days of hire
- Pass assessment with minimum 80% score
- Sign confidentiality agreement
- Complete annual refresher training

### 10.2 Training Content
- FERPA overview and requirements
- Student rights
- Disclosure rules and exceptions
- LMS security practices
- Incident reporting procedures
- Consequences of violations

---

## 11. Violation Response

### 11.1 Reporting
Suspected violations reported to:
- FERPA Officer
- Supervisor
- Compliance Department

### 11.2 Investigation
- Immediate review of incident
- Documentation of findings
- Determination of corrective action
- Notification of affected parties if required

### 11.3 Disciplinary Action
Violations may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Mandatory retraining
- Suspension of access privileges
- Termination of employment
- Legal action if warranted
