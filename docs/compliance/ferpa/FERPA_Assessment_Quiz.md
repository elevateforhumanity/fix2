## Questions

### 1. What does FERPA stand for?

A. Federal Education Records Protection Act  
B. Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act  
C. Federal Enrollment Rights and Privacy Act  
D. Family Education Regulation and Protection Act

**Correct Answer: B**

---

### 2. Which of the following is considered an education record under FERPA?

A. Parking tickets  
B. Student grades  
C. Campus maps  
D. Cafeteria menus

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Education records include any record directly related to a student maintained by the institution. Grades, transcripts, attendance, and LMS activity are all education records.

---

### 3. Who has the right to access education records?

A. Any employer  
B. Other students  
C. The student (or parent if under 18)  
D. Social media followers

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** FERPA rights belong to the student (or parent if student is under 18 and not in postsecondary education). Rights transfer to the student at age 18 or upon enrollment in postsecondary institution.

---

### 4. What type of information requires written consent before disclosure?

A. Directory information  
B. Non-directory information  
C. Public records  
D. Course descriptions

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Non-directory information (grades, attendance, financial aid, etc.) requires written student consent before disclosure to third parties, unless a FERPA exception applies.

---

### 5. Which action violates FERPA?

A. Securely posting grades in LMS  
B. Sharing grades with unauthorized persons  
C. Using role-based access  
D. Encrypting data

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Sharing student information with unauthorized persons without consent or a FERPA exception is a violation. This includes posting grades publicly, discussing students in public, or sharing records with unauthorized individuals.

---

### 6. What must an LMS do to support FERPA compliance?

A. Allow shared logins  
B. Display student grades publicly  
C. Use secure logins and role-based access  
D. Disable audit logs

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** LMS must use secure authentication, role-based access controls, encrypted data transmission, and audit logging to protect student education records.

---

### 7. How often should FERPA training be completed?

A. One time only  
B. Every 10 years  
C. Annually  
D. Never

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** All staff with access to education records must complete FERPA training annually to maintain current knowledge and certification.

---

### 8. Which of the following is directory information (unless student opts out)?

A. Grades  
B. Attendance  
C. Student name  
D. Disability records

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Directory information may include name, program, enrollment dates, and degrees. Students must be notified and given opportunity to opt out. Grades, attendance, and disability information are never directory information.

---

### 9. What should staff do if they suspect a FERPA violation?

A. Ignore it  
B. Post it publicly  
C. Report it according to policy  
D. Share with coworkers

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Suspected violations must be reported immediately to the FERPA Officer, supervisor, or compliance department. Do not ignore violations or attempt to cover them up.

---

### 10. Why is FERPA important for accreditation?

A. It replaces accreditation  
B. It proves financial stability  
C. It demonstrates student privacy compliance  
D. It guarantees funding

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Accreditors require institutions to demonstrate compliance with federal regulations, including FERPA. Proper student privacy protections are essential for accreditation approval and maintenance.

## Bonus Questions (Optional - for 110% score)

### 11. A parent calls requesting their 22-year-old son's grades. What should you do?

A. Provide the grades immediately  
B. Explain that the student must provide consent  
C. Refer them to the Dean  
D. Hang up

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Students 18+ or in postsecondary education have FERPA rights. Parents need written consent from the student unless an exception applies (e.g., student is tax dependent AND institution has policy allowing disclosure).

---

### 12. Can you post grades on the classroom wall using student ID numbers?

A. Yes, ID numbers protect privacy  
B. No, students can identify themselves and others  
C. Yes, if students agree  
D. Only for honor roll students

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Even using ID numbers, students can identify themselves and potentially others. This constitutes public disclosure without consent and violates FERPA.

---

### 13. A workforce partner requests completion data for students. What should you verify first?

A. That the partner has a website  
B. That a data sharing agreement is in place  
C. That students are employed  
D. Nothing - always share

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Data sharing with workforce partners requires a signed data sharing agreement specifying purpose, data elements, security requirements, and prohibition on re-disclosure.

---

### 14. Which of the following is NOT an education record?

A. LMS quiz results  
B. Instructor's personal notes not shared with anyone  
C. Email about a student's performance  
D. Attendance records

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Sole possession notes (personal notes not shared with anyone) are not education records. Once shared, they become education records.

---

### 15. In a health and safety emergency, can you disclose student information?

A. No, never  
B. Yes, but only information necessary to address the emergency  
C. Yes, to anyone who asks  
D. Only with written consent

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The health and safety emergency exception allows disclosure of information necessary to protect health or safety. Disclosure must be documented with justification.
