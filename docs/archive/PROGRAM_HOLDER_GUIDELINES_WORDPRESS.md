**For Licensed Barber Shops in Indianapolis, Indiana**

## Overview

Are you a licensed barber shop owner interested in hosting an apprentice? This U.S. Department of Labor Registered Apprenticeship program allows you to develop talent customized to your shop while contributing to workforce development in Indianapolis.

**Program Number:** [INTraining #10002417](https://intraining.dwd.in.gov/ProgramLocation?ProgramId=10002417)  
**Approved by:** Indiana Department of Workforce Development (DWD)  
**Administered by:** Elevate for Humanity

## What is a Program Holder?

A **Program Holder** (also called a sponsor) is a licensed barber shop that:

- Employs and trains apprentices
- Provides structured on-the-job learning
- Assigns licensed barbers as mentors
- Tracks apprentice hours and progress
- Pays apprentices according to wage progression schedule

## Work Process Schedule

Apprentices must complete **1,500 total hours** covering these competencies:

### Core Skills (On-the-Job Training)

- **Hair Cutting & Styling** (400 hours)
  - Clipper cuts, fades, tapers
  - Scissor cuts, texturing
  - Beard trims and shaping
- **Shaving Services** (200 hours)
  - Straight razor shaves
  - Hot towel treatments
  - Facial grooming

- **Sanitation & Safety** (150 hours)
  - Tool sterilization
  - Workspace sanitation
  - Infection control
  - OSHA compliance

- **Customer Service** (200 hours)
  - Client consultation
  - Communication skills
  - Appointment management
  - Retail sales

- **Shop Operations** (150 hours)
  - Cash handling
  - Inventory management
  - Scheduling
  - Professional conduct

- **Advanced Techniques** (200 hours)
  - Design work
  - Color services
  - Chemical treatments
  - Specialty services

- **Related Technical Instruction** (200 hours)
  - Classroom theory
  - Indiana barber laws
  - Business practices
  - Exam preparation

## Wage Progression Requirements

All registered apprenticeships must include wage progression. Apprentices earn from day one with scheduled raises:

| Period             | Minimum Wage | Skill Level                               |
| ------------------ | ------------ | ----------------------------------------- |
| **Months 1-6**     | $12-15/hour  | Entry level, basic cuts under supervision |
| **Months 7-12**    | $15-18/hour  | Intermediate skills, more independence    |
| **Months 13-18**   | $18-22/hour  | Advanced skills, minimal supervision      |
| **Post-Licensure** | $25-40/hour+ | Licensed barber, full independence        |

**Note:** Wages are negotiable between shop and apprentice but must meet minimum progression standards. Many shops also allow apprentices to keep tips.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many apprentices can I host?

Most shops start with one apprentice. You can host multiple apprentices if you have adequate supervision (1 licensed barber per apprentice recommended).

### What if the apprentice doesn't work out?

You can terminate the apprenticeship with proper notice. We'll work with you to address issues and potentially match you with a different apprentice.

### Do I have to pay for their classroom training?

No. Related Technical Instruction (RTI) is covered through WIOA funding or other sources. You only pay the apprentice's hourly wage for shop work.

### What if I've never trained an apprentice before?

We provide full support including mentor training, documentation templates, and ongoing guidance. You're not alone in this process.

### How long does the apprenticeship last?

Typically 12-18 months to complete 1,500 hours. Timeline varies based on full-time vs part-time schedule.

### Can the apprentice work for me after they get licensed?

Yes! Many apprentices continue working at their training shop after licensure. You have first opportunity to hire them.
