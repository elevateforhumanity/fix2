## 3. Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. Perform core barbering services (cuts, shaves, facial hair design, basic skincare) to state standards.  
2. Demonstrate safe sanitation, disinfection, and infection-control procedures.  
3. Apply state laws and rules related to barbering, licensing, and client safety.  
4. Communicate professionally with clients, manage bookings, and handle basic shop etiquette.  
5. Document apprenticeship hours accurately and work within a real barbershop environment.  
6. Pass the state barber theory and practical licensing examinations.

## 4. Course Structure

### 4.1 Units Overview

- **Unit 1 – Orientation & Professionalism**  
  Focus: Intro to the trade, expectations, professionalism, basic tools  
  Hours: 40 (Classroom/Lab)

- **Unit 2 – Safety, Sanitation & Infection Control**  
  Focus: State sanitation rules, infection control, bloodborne pathogens  
  Hours: 80 (Classroom/Lab)

- **Unit 3 – Haircutting Fundamentals**  
  Focus: Tools, sectioning, basic cuts, fades, tapers  
  Hours: 200 (Lab/OJT)

- **Unit 4 – Shaving & Facial Hair Design**  
  Focus: Razor safety, shaving techniques, beard design  
  Hours: 150 (Lab/OJT)

- **Unit 5 – Chemical Services (If state-allowed)**  
  Focus: Relaxers, color basics, perms  
  Hours: 150 (Classroom/Lab)

- **Unit 6 – Client Care, Shop Operations & Business Basics**  
  Focus: Customer service, booking, retail, shop policies  
  Hours: 80 (Classroom)

- **Unit 7 – State Law, Exam Prep & Review**  
  Focus: Laws, rules, test prep  
  Hours: 100 (Classroom/Online)

- **Ongoing – Barber Apprenticeship OJT**  
  Focus: On-the-job hours in an approved barbershop  
  Hours: Remainder to reach total required hours (e.g., 1500)

---

### 4.2 Detailed Sample Unit Breakdown

#### Unit 2 – Safety, Sanitation & Infection Control

- **Hours:** 80 (40 Classroom, 40 Lab)  
- **Objectives:**
  - Identify common pathogens and how they spread in a barbershop.  
  - Demonstrate correct sanitation and disinfection of tools and workstations.  
  - Comply with Indiana (or your state's) health and safety regulations.  

- **Lessons in this Unit:**
  1. Lesson 2.1 – Introduction to Infection Control  
     - Content: LMS video + reading + short quiz  
     - Activities: Group discussion, scenario review  
     - Assessment: Online quiz (70%+ to pass)  

  2. Lesson 2.2 – Disinfection Procedures & Tool Maintenance  
     - Content: Step-by-step videos + PDF checklist  
     - Activities: In-lab demo, supervised practice  
     - Assessment: Skills checklist (instructor mark-off)  

  3. Lesson 2.3 – State Health Codes & Shop Inspections  
     - Content: LMS module with law excerpts  
     - Activities: Mock inspection, write-up assignment  
     - Assessment: Written assignment + oral Q&A

## 5. Assessments & Grading

- **Assessment Types:**
  - Online quizzes for theory units (via LMS)  
  - Practical skills checklists (haircuts, shaves, sanitation)  
  - OJT supervisor evaluations  
  - Final theory exam (state-aligned)  
  - Mock state practical exam  

- **Minimum Passing Requirements:**
  - Attendance: 90% of required hours  
  - Theory: 75%+ average on quizzes and exams  
  - Skills: All critical skills rated "competent"  
  - OJT: Satisfactory supervisor evaluations  

- **Tracking in LMS:**
  - Quizzes and exams recorded automatically  
  - Skills checklists uploaded/scanned or entered  
  - OJT hours logged weekly  
  - Completion status synced to:
    - Student profile  
    - Employer/agency reports
