## Phase 2 — New State Setup (Repeatable Process)

### Timeline: 30-60 Days Per State

---

### Step 1: Sponsor Registration (Week 1-2)

**Action Items:**

- [ ] Identify state apprenticeship agency (SAA) or use USDOL
- [ ] Submit sponsor application
- [ ] Provide organizational documentation
- [ ] Specify initial trades/occupations
- [ ] Attend orientation (if required)

**Documents Needed:**

- Articles of incorporation
- EIN documentation
- Organizational chart
- Staff qualifications
- Sample training plans
- Employer agreements

**Contacts:**

- State Apprenticeship Director
- USDOL Regional Office (if no SAA)

**Deliverable:** Sponsor approval letter

---

### Step 2: State Rules Configuration (Week 2-3)

**Action Items:**

- [ ] Research state-specific requirements
- [ ] Document wage progression rules
- [ ] Identify RTI hour requirements
- [ ] Map credential requirements
- [ ] Configure rules engine

**State-Specific Research:**

- Minimum wage laws
- Apprentice-to-journeyworker ratios
- Safety training requirements
- Industry licensing requirements
- Reporting frequencies

**Platform Configuration:**

- Update rules engine with state parameters
- Configure wage progression schedules
- Set up state-specific forms
- Enable state reporting templates

**Deliverable:** State configuration document

---

### Step 3: RTI Partner Identification (Week 3-4)

**Action Items:**

- [ ] Identify community colleges
- [ ] Identify trade schools
- [ ] Identify online providers
- [ ] Negotiate partnerships
- [ ] Execute agreements

**Partner Criteria:**

- Accredited/approved by state
- Relevant curriculum available
- Flexible scheduling (evening/online)
- Affordable pricing
- Willing to coordinate with sponsor

**Agreement Elements:**

- Tuition rates
- Enrollment process
- Progress reporting
- Credential issuance
- Payment terms

**Deliverable:** RTI partner agreements

---

### Step 4: Funding Alignment (Week 4-5)

**Action Items:**

- [ ] Map WIOA equivalents
- [ ] Identify state workforce grants
- [ ] Research OJT reimbursement rates
- [ ] Verify WOTC processing
- [ ] Document funding pathways

**Funding Sources to Research:**

- WIOA Title I (Adult, DW, Youth)
- State workforce development grants
- Industry-specific grants
- Tax credits (state and federal)
- Employer incentives

**Documentation:**

- Eligibility criteria
- Application processes
- Reimbursement rates
- Reporting requirements
- Payment timelines

**Deliverable:** Funding alignment guide

---

### Step 5: Tenant Activation (Week 5-6)

**Action Items:**

- [ ] Create tenant in platform
- [ ] Configure state rules
- [ ] Set up user roles
- [ ] Import employer templates
- [ ] Enable reporting

**Platform Setup:**

- Tenant name and branding
- State-specific forms
- Wage progression schedules
- Compliance checklists
- Reporting templates

**User Configuration:**

- Admin accounts
- Employer portal access
- Workforce agency read-only access
- Support contacts

**Deliverable:** Live tenant ready for employers

---

### Step 6: Employer Onboarding (Week 6+)

**Action Items:**

- [ ] Identify target employers

**Employer Recruitment:**

### White-Label Licensing

**Model:**
Workforce agencies, community colleges, or intermediaries license the platform to operate their own apprenticeship programs under their brand.

**What's Included:**

- Full platform access
- State configuration
- Training and support
- Compliance infrastructure
- Reporting tools

**What's Not Included:**

- Employer recruitment (client responsibility)
- Direct participant services (client responsibility)
- Funding (client secures)

**Pricing:**

- Setup fee: $5k-$25k (based on complexity)
- Monthly license: $2.5k-$7.5k (based on volume)
- Per-apprentice fee: $50-$150/month

## State-Specific Considerations

### Ohio

- **SAA:** Yes (Ohio State Apprenticeship Council)
- **ETPL:** Required for WIOA funding
- **Unique Rules:** Apprentice-to-journeyworker ratios strictly enforced
- **Funding:** OhioMeansJobs, TechCred
- **Key Partners:** OhioMeansJobs centers, community colleges

### Kentucky

- **SAA:** No (use USDOL)
- **ETPL:** Required
- **Unique Rules:** Work-based learning emphasis
- **Funding:** KCTCS Work Ready Skills, WIOA
- **Key Partners:** Kentucky Career Centers, KCTCS

### Illinois

- **SAA:** Yes (Illinois Department of Labor)
- **ETPL:** Required
- **Unique Rules:** Prevailing wage considerations for construction
- **Funding:** WIOA, Workforce Innovation Fund
- **Key Partners:** American Job Centers, City Colleges of Chicago

### Texas

- **SAA:** No (use USDOL)
- **ETPL:** Required for Workforce Solutions
- **Unique Rules:** TWC (Texas Workforce Commission) oversight
- **Funding:** Skills Development Fund, WIOA
- **Key Partners:** Workforce Solutions offices, community colleges

## Risk Mitigation

### Regulatory Risk

**Risk:** State rules change, affecting compliance  
**Mitigation:** Monitor regulatory updates, maintain flexible rules engine, engage with state agencies

### Funding Risk

**Risk:** Workforce funding reduced or eliminated  
**Mitigation:** Diversify revenue (employer fees, licensing), build employer value beyond funding

### Employer Risk

**Risk:** Employers drop out or fail to train properly  
**Mitigation:** Thorough vetting, clear agreements, ongoing support, backup employer pipeline

### Participant Risk

**Risk:** Low retention or completion rates  
**Mitigation:** Intensive case management, supportive services, employer training on mentorship

### Technology Risk

**Risk:** Platform downtime or data loss  
**Mitigation:** Cloud infrastructure, automated backups, disaster recovery plan, SOC 2 controls
