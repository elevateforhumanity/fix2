# Phlebotomy Technician
## Program Syllabus

**Provider:** Elevate for Humanity  
**ETPL Provider ID:** 10000949  
**Program Code:** PHLEB-TECH  
**Duration:** 4-8 Weeks (120-160 Hours)  
**Delivery Method:** Classroom + Clinical Rotations  
**Funding:** WRG • WIOA • Workforce Grants  

---

## Program Overview

Launch your healthcare career in just 4-8 weeks! Learn proper blood draw techniques, patient interaction, lab safety, and specimen handling. Hands-on program includes clinical rotations at real healthcare facilities. Graduate ready for immediate employment in hospitals, clinics, blood banks, and diagnostic labs with national certification.

---

## Learning Objectives

1. Perform venipuncture using proper technique
2. Collect capillary blood specimens
3. Process and handle specimens correctly
4. Apply infection control and safety protocols
5. Interact professionally with patients
6. Use phlebotomy equipment and supplies
7. Maintain accurate documentation
8. Recognize and respond to complications

---

## Course Outline

### Module 1: Introduction to Phlebotomy (20 hours)
- Role and responsibilities of phlebotomist
- Healthcare delivery systems
- Medical terminology
- Anatomy and physiology (circulatory system)
- Legal and ethical issues
- HIPAA and patient confidentiality

### Module 2: Safety & Infection Control (20 hours)
- Standard precautions
- Bloodborne pathogens
- Personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Hand hygiene
- Sharps safety and disposal
- Exposure control plan
- OSHA regulations

### Module 3: Venipuncture Techniques (40 hours)
- Venipuncture equipment and supplies
- Vein selection and site preparation
- Evacuated tube system
- Syringe method
- Butterfly needle technique
- Order of draw
- Specimen labeling and handling

### Module 4: Capillary Collection (20 hours)
- Capillary puncture sites
- Finger stick technique
- Heel stick (infant collection)
- Capillary collection devices
- Point-of-care testing
- Glucose monitoring

### Module 5: Special Collections & Procedures (20 hours)
- Blood cultures
- Glucose tolerance tests
- Therapeutic drug monitoring
- Pediatric collections
- Geriatric considerations
- Difficult draws and problem-solving

### Module 6: Specimen Processing & Quality (20 hours)
- Specimen handling and transport
- Centrifugation procedures
- Quality control and quality assurance
- Pre-analytical variables
- Specimen rejection criteria
- Laboratory information systems

### Module 7: Patient Interaction & Professionalism (10 hours)
- Communication skills
- Patient identification
- Managing anxious patients
- Cultural sensitivity
- Professional appearance and conduct
- Customer service

### Module 8: Clinical Practicum (40-80 hours)
- Supervised venipuncture in clinical setting
- Minimum 100 successful blood draws
- Patient interaction
- Specimen processing
- Professional behavior
- Preceptor evaluation

## Required Materials

- Scrubs and closed-toe shoes
- Watch with second hand
- Phlebotomy textbook (provided)
- Lab supplies (provided during training)
- Background check and drug screening
- Immunization records (TB, Hepatitis B, MMR, Varicella)
- CPR certification (obtained during program)

---

## Instructor Qualifications

- Certified phlebotomist (CPT, PBT, or equivalent)
- Minimum 3 years clinical phlebotomy experience
- Teaching experience or adult education background
- Current CPR/BLS certification
