## Program Overview

Make a difference in recovery! Train to provide peer support services in behavioral health, addiction recovery, and mental health settings. Learn trauma-informed care, crisis intervention, and recovery coaching. No prior healthcare experience required.

---

## Learning Objectives

1. Apply peer support principles in behavioral health settings
2. Use trauma-informed care approaches
3. Provide crisis intervention and de-escalation
4. Support individuals with mental health challenges
5. Connect clients to community resources
6. Maintain professional boundaries and ethics
7. Document peer support services
8. Advocate for individuals in recovery

---

## Course Outline

### Module 1: Introduction to Peer Support (20 hours)
- History and philosophy of peer support
- Recovery-oriented systems of care
- Peer support core competencies
- Ethics and professional boundaries
- Self-care and wellness
- Cultural competency

### Module 2: Mental Health & Behavioral Health (25 hours)
- Mental health conditions overview
- Co-occurring disorders
- Trauma and its effects
- Stages of change
- Recovery models and pathways
- Person-centered care

### Module 3: Trauma-Informed Care (20 hours)
- Understanding trauma
- Trauma-informed principles
- Creating safe environments
- Recognizing trauma responses
- Avoiding re-traumatization
- Resilience and healing

### Module 4: Peer Support Skills (25 hours)
- Active listening and empathy
- Motivational interviewing basics
- Goal setting and action planning
- Problem-solving strategies
- Strengths-based approaches
- Recovery planning

### Module 5: Crisis Intervention (20 hours)
- Recognizing crisis situations
- De-escalation techniques
- Safety assessment
- Crisis response protocols
- Suicide prevention
- Emergency resources

### Module 6: Community Resources & Navigation (15 hours)
- Mental health services
- Substance use treatment
- Housing and employment resources
- Healthcare and benefits
- Legal system navigation
- Support groups and recovery communities

### Module 7: Professional Practice (15 hours)
- HIPAA and confidentiality
- Documentation and record keeping
- Working within treatment teams
- Supervision and consultation
- Professional development
- Certification requirements

### Module 8: Supervised Practicum (20-40 hours)
- Observation of peer support services
- Supervised client interactions
- Case study discussions
- Skills practice and feedback
- Professional portfolio development

## Indiana Certification Requirements

To apply for state certification, candidates must:
1. Be at least 18 years old
2. Have high school diploma or equivalent
3. Have lived experience with mental health or recovery
4. Complete approved training program (this program)
5. Pass background check
6. Submit application to Indiana DMHA
7. Maintain continuing education
