# Barber Apprenticeship
## Program Syllabus

**Provider:** Elevate for Humanity  
**ETPL Provider ID:** 10000949  
**Program Code:** BARBER-APPR  
**Duration:** 12-18 Months (1,500 Hours)  
**Delivery Method:** Registered Apprenticeship (Barbershop + Classroom)  
**Funding:** Registered Apprenticeship • WIOA • WRG  

---

## Program Overview

Earn while you learn! State-approved apprenticeship places students in real barbershops where they master fades, tapers, razor work, and client service while building hours toward Indiana barber licensure. Perfect for career changers and re-entry participants. Get paid on-the-job training, business coaching, and a clear path to owning your own chair or shop.

---

## Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion, students will be able to:

1. Perform all barbering services including haircuts, shaves, and beard trims
2. Execute advanced cutting techniques (fades, tapers, designs)
3. Use barbering tools safely and effectively (clippers, shears, razors)
4. Apply sanitation and infection control protocols per Indiana State Board
5. Provide professional client consultations and customer service
6. Manage barbershop operations and business finances
7. Build and maintain a client base
8. Pass Indiana State Board of Barber Examiners licensing exam

---

## Course Outline

### Phase 1: Foundations (Months 1-3, 375 Hours)

#### Classroom Instruction (75 hours)
- Indiana Barber Law and Rules
- Sanitation and infection control
- Anatomy and physiology (scalp, hair, skin)
- Barbering theory and principles
- Tool identification and maintenance
- Client consultation techniques
- Professional ethics and conduct

#### On-the-Job Training (300 hours)
- Shop orientation and safety
- Tool handling and maintenance
- Shampooing and scalp treatments
- Basic clipper work
- Observation of licensed barbers
- Client interaction and communication
- Shop cleanliness and sanitation

### Phase 2: Core Skills Development (Months 4-9, 750 Hours)

#### Classroom Instruction (75 hours)
- Advanced cutting techniques
- Facial hair design and grooming
- Chemical services (relaxers, texturizers)
- Skin care and shaving
- Color theory and application
- Business management basics
- Customer service excellence

#### On-the-Job Training (675 hours)
- Men's haircuts (all styles)
- Clipper techniques (fades, tapers, blends)
- Scissor-over-comb techniques
- Beard trimming and shaping
- Straight razor shaves
- Chemical service application
- Building client relationships
- Appointment scheduling

### Phase 3: Mastery & Business (Months 10-18, 375 Hours)

#### Classroom Instruction (75 hours)
- Advanced design and artistry
- Trend analysis and style adaptation
- Marketing and social media
- Financial management
- Booth rental vs. employment
- Opening your own shop
- State Board exam preparation

#### On-the-Job Training (300 hours)
- Independent client services
- Advanced fades and designs
- Specialty services (hot towel shaves, facials)
- Retail product sales
- Client retention strategies
- Mentoring junior apprentices
- Full barbershop operations

## Assessment Methods

- **Monthly Progress Reports:** Employer evaluation of skills
- **Classroom Exams:** 70% passing required
- **Practical Skills Assessments:** Demonstrated competency
- **Client Service Evaluations:** Customer satisfaction
- **Portfolio Development:** Before/after photos of work
- **Mock State Board Exam:** Practice for licensure exam
- **Final Evaluation:** Comprehensive skills demonstration

---

## Grading Scale

- **Exceeds Standards:** Advanced proficiency, ready for licensure
- **Meets Standards:** Competent performance, on track
- **Needs Improvement:** Additional practice required
- **Unsatisfactory:** Does not meet minimum standards

**Progression Requirements:** Must meet standards in each phase to advance

## Instructor/Mentor Qualifications

**Classroom Instructors:**
- Licensed barber instructor (Indiana State Board)
- Minimum 5 years barbering experience
- Teaching credential or adult education experience

**On-the-Job Mentors:**
- Licensed master barber (Indiana)
- Minimum 3 years professional experience
- Approved by Indiana State Board as apprenticeship sponsor
- Background check and good standing with State Board

---

## Apprenticeship Sponsor Requirements

Participating barbershops must:
- Hold valid Indiana barbershop license
- Employ at least one licensed master barber
- Maintain sanitation standards per State Board
- Provide safe working environment
- Register with U.S. Department of Labor as apprenticeship sponsor
- Commit to mentoring and training apprentice

---

## Compensation

Apprentices earn wages while training:
- **Phase 1 (Months 1-3):** 40-50% of licensed barber rate
- **Phase 2 (Months 4-9):** 50-70% of licensed barber rate
- **Phase 3 (Months 10-18):** 70-90% of licensed barber rate

**Additional Income:** Tips from clients (apprentice keeps 100%)

## Indiana State Board Licensure

To obtain Indiana barber license, graduates must:
1. Complete 1,500 hours of approved apprenticeship
2. Be at least 18 years old
3. Have high school diploma or equivalent
4. Pass State Board written exam (75%)
5. Pass State Board practical exam
6. Pay licensing fees
7. Maintain continuing education (renewal)
