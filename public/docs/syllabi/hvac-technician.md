# HVAC Technician
## Program Syllabus

**Provider:** Elevate for Humanity  
**ETPL Provider ID:** 10000949  
**Program Code:** HVAC-TECH  
**Duration:** 4-9 Months (320-720 Hours)  
**Delivery Method:** Hybrid (Lab + Field Training)  
**Funding:** Workforce Grants • Employer Sponsors • WIOA  

---

## Program Overview

Start a high-paying skilled trade career. Learn to install, repair, and maintain heating, cooling, and refrigeration systems through hands-on lab work and real job sites. Program covers EPA certification, electrical basics, troubleshooting, and customer service. Graduate ready for immediate employment with HVAC companies, property management, or start your own business.

---

## Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion, students will be able to:

1. Install, maintain, and repair residential and commercial HVAC systems
2. Diagnose and troubleshoot heating and cooling equipment failures
3. Apply EPA regulations for refrigerant handling (Section 608 certification)
4. Read and interpret HVAC blueprints and schematics
5. Use HVAC tools and testing equipment safely and effectively
6. Perform electrical troubleshooting on HVAC systems
7. Provide professional customer service and system recommendations
8. Follow OSHA safety standards for HVAC work

---

## Course Outline

### Module 1: HVAC Fundamentals & Safety (40 hours)
- Introduction to heating, ventilation, and air conditioning
- HVAC career pathways and industry overview
- OSHA 10-Hour Construction Safety
- Personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Tool safety and proper usage
- Ladder safety and fall protection
- Electrical safety basics

### Module 2: Refrigeration Principles (60 hours)
- Refrigeration cycle and thermodynamics
- Pressure-temperature relationships
- Refrigerant types and properties
- Compressors, condensers, evaporators, metering devices
- Refrigerant piping and fittings
- Charging and recovery procedures
- EPA Section 608 certification preparation

### Module 3: Heating Systems (60 hours)
- Gas furnace operation and components
- Electric heating systems
- Heat pumps and dual-fuel systems
- Combustion analysis and efficiency
- Venting and exhaust systems
- Thermostat wiring and controls
- Troubleshooting heating problems

### Module 4: Air Conditioning Systems (60 hours)
- Split system components and operation
- Package units and rooftop units
- Ductwork design and installation
- Airflow measurement and balancing
- Condensate drainage systems
- Refrigerant charging methods
- AC troubleshooting and repair

### Module 5: Electrical for HVAC (80 hours)
- AC and DC electrical theory
- Ohm's Law and electrical calculations
- Reading electrical schematics and wiring diagrams
- Multimeter usage and testing
- Contactors, relays, and capacitors
- Motors and motor controls
- Troubleshooting electrical circuits

### Module 6: HVAC Controls & Automation (40 hours)
- Thermostat types and installation
- Digital and programmable controls
- Building automation systems (BAS) basics
- Zone control systems
- Smart home HVAC integration
- Control troubleshooting

### Module 7: Installation & Service Procedures (80 hours)
- Equipment sizing and load calculations
- Installation best practices
- Brazing and soldering techniques
- Leak detection and repair
- System startup and commissioning
- Preventive maintenance procedures
- Service documentation and reporting

### Module 8: Customer Service & Business Operations (40 hours)
- Professional communication skills
- Customer service excellence
- Estimating and pricing
- Service agreements and contracts
- Business licensing and insurance
- Marketing your HVAC business
- Ethics and professionalism

### Module 9: Hands-On Lab Training (120-240 hours)
- Equipment disassembly and reassembly
- Refrigerant recovery and charging
- System installation projects
- Troubleshooting simulations
- Real equipment diagnostics
- Tool proficiency development

### Module 10: Field Training Externship (120-240 hours)
- Supervised work with HVAC company
- Residential service calls
- Commercial equipment maintenance
- Customer interaction
- Job site safety and professionalism
- Employer evaluation

## Assessment Methods

- **Module Exams:** 70% passing required
- **Hands-On Skills Assessments:** Demonstrated competency required
- **EPA Section 608 Certification Exam:** Required for program completion
- **OSHA 10 Certification:** Required for field training
- **Lab Performance Evaluations:** Instructor assessment
- **Field Training Evaluation:** Employer/supervisor assessment
- **Final Comprehensive Exam:** 75% passing required

---

## Grading Scale

- **A:** 90-100% - Excellent technical skills
- **B:** 80-89% - Good technical proficiency
- **C:** 70-79% - Satisfactory performance
- **F:** Below 70% - Does not meet standards

**Minimum passing grade:** 70% theory, demonstrated competency in all hands-on skills
