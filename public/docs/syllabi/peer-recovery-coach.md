## Program Overview

Support individuals in addiction recovery! Become a Certified Peer Recovery Coach and help others navigate their recovery journey. Learn motivational interviewing, relapse prevention, and community resources. Justice-involved individuals welcome.

---

## Learning Objectives

1. Apply peer support principles in recovery settings
2. Use motivational interviewing techniques
3. Develop recovery plans with clients
4. Connect clients to community resources
5. Recognize and respond to relapse warning signs
6. Maintain professional boundaries and ethics
7. Document peer support services accurately
8. Advocate for individuals in recovery

---

## Course Outline

### Module 1: Foundations of Peer Support (30 hours)
- History and philosophy of peer support
- Recovery-oriented systems of care
- Peer support core competencies
- Ethics and professional boundaries
- Self-care and wellness
- Cultural competency and diversity

### Module 2: Addiction & Recovery (30 hours)
- Substance use disorders overview
- Brain science of addiction
- Stages of change model
- Recovery pathways and models
- Co-occurring disorders
- Trauma-informed care

### Module 3: Peer Support Skills (40 hours)
- Active listening and empathy
- Motivational interviewing basics
- Goal setting and action planning
- Problem-solving strategies
- Crisis intervention basics
- Conflict resolution

### Module 4: Relapse Prevention (20 hours)
- Relapse warning signs
- Triggers and coping strategies
- Developing relapse prevention plans
- Supporting clients through setbacks
- Building recovery capital
- Long-term recovery maintenance

### Module 5: Community Resources & Navigation (20 hours)
- Treatment and recovery services
- Housing and employment resources
- Healthcare and benefits navigation
- Legal system navigation
- Family and social support
- Recovery community organizations

### Module 6: Documentation & Professional Practice (20 hours)
- HIPAA and confidentiality
- Progress notes and documentation
- Supervision and consultation
- Working within treatment teams
- Professional development
- Certification requirements

### Module 7: Supervised Practicum (20 hours)
- Observation of peer support services
- Supervised client interactions
- Case study discussions
- Skills practice and feedback
- Professional portfolio development

## Indiana CPRC Certification Requirements

To apply for state certification, candidates must:
1. Be at least 18 years old
2. Have high school diploma or equivalent
3. Have personal recovery experience (1+ year)
4. Complete approved training program (this program)
5. Pass background check
6. Submit application to Indiana DMHA
7. Maintain continuing education
