# Grant Revenue Structure - Elevate for Humanity

## üí∞ Revenue Share Models

### Model 0: Micro Programs (Partner Catalog Courses)
**Program Holder Receives**: 50% of revenue after credentialed partner fees, tests, and supplies

**What Are Micro Programs?**
Micro programs are SHORT courses pulled from credentialed partners' catalogs and course libraries. These are pre-packaged courses from established training providers.

**Micro Programs Include**:
- Phlebotomy Technician (from partner catalog)
- EKG Technician (from partner catalog)
- Pharmacy Technician (from partner catalog)
- Dental Assistant (from partner catalog)
- Patient Care Technician (from partner catalog)
- Sterile Processing (from partner catalog)
- Any other short courses from partner libraries

**NOT Micro Programs** (These are ETPL/Apprenticeship Programs):
- CPR Certification (ETPL program)
- Esthetician (Apprenticeship program)
- Culinary Arts Apprenticeship (Apprenticeship program)
- Financial Literacy (ETPL program)
- Peer Recovery Coach (ETPL program)
- Tax Office Certification (ETPL program)

**Example - $1,000 Micro Program**:
- Student pays: $1,000
- Credentialed partner fees: -$150
- Test/exam fees: -$100
- Supplies/materials: -$50
- Net revenue: $700
- **Program holder gets: $350 (50%)**
- **Elevate keeps: $350 (50%)**

**Payment Schedule**:
- 30% at student start
- 30% at midpoint
- 40% at completion

**Markup Structure**:
- Base cost from partner: $500 (partner fees, tests, supplies)
- 50% markup split: $250 to program holder, $250 to Elevate
- Total price to student: $1,000

**Key Difference**:
Micro programs are PRE-BUILT courses from partner catalogs. You're reselling their content with a 50% markup split.

## üí∞ Revenue Share Models

### Model 1: ETPL Programs (Eligible Training Provider List)
**Program Holder Receives**: 1/3 of net revenue after credentialed partner fees, books, tests, and supplies

**What Are ETPL Programs?**
ETPL programs are state-approved training programs on Indiana's Eligible Training Provider List. These are YOUR programs that you deliver.

**ETPL Programs Include**:
- CPR Certification
- Financial Literacy
- Peer Recovery Coach
- Tax Office Certification
- Medical Assistant
- CNA Training
- HVAC Technician
- Welding
- And other state-approved programs

**Key Difference**:
ETPL programs are YOUR training programs. You create the curriculum (following master syllabus), deliver instruction, and manage students.

**Example - $3,000 ETPL Program**:
- Student pays: $3,000
- Credentialed partner fees: -$200
- Books/supplies: -$300
- Tests: -$100
- Net revenue: $2,400
- **Program holder gets: $800 (1/3)**
- **Elevate keeps: $1,600 (2/3)**

**Payment Schedule**:
- 25% at student start: $200
- 35% at midpoint: $280
- 40% at completion: $320

### Model 2: Apprenticeship Programs
**Program Holder Receives**: 1/3 of net revenue after credentialed partner fees, books, tests, and supplies

**What Are Apprenticeship Programs?**
Registered apprenticeship programs combining classroom instruction with on-the-job training. These require hour tracking and employer partnerships.

**Apprenticeship Programs Include**:
- Barber Apprenticeship (1,500 hours)
- Esthetician Apprenticeship (600 hours)
- Culinary Arts Apprenticeship
- HVAC Apprenticeship
- Electrical Apprenticeship
- Plumbing Apprenticeship
- And other registered apprenticeships

**Key Features**:
- Hour tracking required
- Employer/shop partnership
- State licensure pathway
- Earn while you learn
- Longer duration (6-24 months)

**Example - $5,000 Apprenticeship Program**:
- Grant funding: $5,000
- Partner fees: -$300
- Books/supplies: -$400
- Tests/licensing: -$300
- Net revenue: $4,000
- **Program holder gets: $1,333 (1/3)**
- **Elevate keeps: $2,667 (2/3)**

**Payment Schedule**:
- 25% at student start: $333
- 35% at midpoint: $467
- 40% at completion: $533

### Model 3: Full Training Programs (Grant-Funded)
**Program Holder Receives**: 1/3 of net revenue after credentialed partner fees, books, tests, and supplies

**Payment Schedule (Performance-Based)**:
- **25% at Student Start** - Upon enrollment and first day of attendance
- **35% at Midpoint** - When student reaches 50% program completion
- **40% at Completion** - Upon successful program completion with documentation

**Example - $3,000 Program**:
- Student pays: $3,000
- Books & fees: -$300
- Net revenue: $2,700
- Program holder total (1/3): $900

**Payment Breakdown**:
- At start (25%): $225
- At midpoint (35%): $315
- At completion (40%): $360
- **Total**: $900

**Required Documentation**:
- Start: Enrollment form, attendance verification, signed syllabus
- Midpoint: Progress report, attendance log, skills assessment
- Completion: Certificate of completion, final grades, placement documentation

---

### Model 2: Grant-Written Programs (WIOA, WRG, Federal Grants)
**Program Holder Receives**: 1/3 of grant funding per student

**Payment Schedule (Performance-Based)**:
- **25% at Student Start** - Upon enrollment and first day of attendance
- **35% at Midpoint** - When student reaches 50% program completion
- **40% at Completion** - Upon successful program completion with all documentation

**If Elevate Writes the Grant**:
- Program holder receives: 1/3 of grant funding (performance-based)
- Elevate receives: 2/3 of grant funding
- Elevate handles: Compliance, reporting, administration

**If Program Holder Writes the Grant**:
- Program holder receives: 1/3 of grant funding (performance-based) + writing fee
- Elevate receives: 2/3 of grant funding
- Elevate handles: Compliance, reporting, administration

**Example - $100,000 WIOA Grant (20 students @ $5,000 each)**:
- Total grant: $100,000
- Per student funding: $5,000
- Program holder share per student (1/3): $1,667

**Payment Breakdown Per Student**:
- At start (25%): $417
- At midpoint (35%): $583
- At completion (40%): $667
- **Total per student**: $1,667

**Required Documentation for Each Payment**:

**At Start (25% - $417)**:
- Enrollment application with signature
- WIOA eligibility determination
- Attendance verification (first week)
- Signed training agreement
- Orientation completion certificate

**At Midpoint (35% - $583)**:
- Attendance log (50% completion)
- Progress report with grades
- Skills assessment results
- Instructor evaluation
- Mid-program counseling notes
- Continued eligibility verification

**At Completion (40% - $667)**:
- Certificate of completion
- Final transcript with grades
- Attendance record (80%+ required)
- Industry certification (if applicable)
- Exit interview documentation
- Job placement verification (if placed)
- PIRL data submission
- Final compliance checklist

## üìã Grant Types & Revenue Split

### WIOA (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act)
- **Split**: 1/3 to program holder, 2/3 to Elevate
- **Who writes**: Either party
- **Compliance**: Elevate manages PIRL reporting
- **Duration**: Typically 1-2 years

### WRG (Workforce Ready Grants)
- **Split**: 1/3 to program holder, 2/3 to Elevate
- **Who writes**: Either party
- **Compliance**: Elevate manages state reporting
- **Duration**: Typically 6-12 months

### Federal Grants (DOL, HHS, etc.)
- **Split**: 1/3 to program holder, 2/3 to Elevate
- **Who writes**: Either party
- **Compliance**: Elevate manages federal reporting
- **Duration**: Varies (1-5 years)

### Foundation Grants
- **Split**: 1/3 to program holder, 2/3 to Elevate
- **Who writes**: Either party
- **Compliance**: Elevate manages foundation reporting
- **Duration**: Varies
