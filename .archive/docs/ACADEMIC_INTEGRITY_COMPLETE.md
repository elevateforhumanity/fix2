## ðŸ“– POLICY CONTENT

### What Constitutes Academic Dishonesty

#### 1. Plagiarism âœ…

**Definition:** Using someone else's words, ideas, or work without proper attribution.

**Examples Covered:**

- Copying text without citation
- Submitting someone else's work
- Paraphrasing without credit
- Using AI-generated content without disclosure
- Copying another student's work
- Purchasing papers online

#### 2. Cheating âœ…

**Definition:** Using unauthorized materials or assistance during assessments.

**Examples Covered:**

- Using notes during closed-book exams
- Looking at another student's exam
- Sharing answers during tests
- Using unauthorized technology
- Taking photos of exams
- Having someone else take an exam

#### 3. Unauthorized Collaboration âœ…

**Definition:** Working with others when individual work is required.

**Examples Covered:**

- Completing individual assignments as a group
- Sharing completed work
- Dividing work on individual projects
- Allowing others to copy your work

**Note:** Policy clarifies when collaboration IS allowed.

#### 4. Falsification of Records âœ…

**Definition:** Altering or fabricating academic records or documentation.

**Examples Covered:**

- Forging signatures
- Altering grades or transcripts
- Fabricating data or research
- Falsifying attendance records
- Submitting false documentation
- Misrepresenting hours worked

#### 5. Multiple Submissions âœ…

**Definition:** Submitting the same work for multiple courses without permission.

**Policy:** Students must obtain explicit permission from all instructors.

## âš–ï¸ CONSEQUENCES & DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES

### Three-Strike System âœ…

#### First Offense:

- Written warning in student file
- Zero grade on assignment/assessment
- Meeting with instructor and advisor
- Academic integrity workshop required
- Probationary status for remainder of term

#### Second Offense:

- Failure (F) in the course
- Academic probation for one full term
- Meeting with Dean of Students
- Notation on transcript (removable upon graduation)
- Possible suspension from program

#### Third Offense:

- Dismissal from the program
- Permanent notation on transcript
- No refund of tuition or fees
- Ineligibility for re-admission
- Notification to licensing boards (if applicable)

### Severe Violations:

**Immediate dismissal** for egregious violations such as:

- Falsifying clinical records
- Forging signatures
- Systematic cheating
