# HEALTHCARE / CLINIC SITE MOU

## Between Elevate for Humanity and \***\*\*\*\*\***\_\_\***\*\*\*\*\***

This Healthcare / Clinic Site Memorandum of Understanding ("MOU") is entered into by and between:

- **Elevate for Humanity** ("Elevate"), and
- **\*\*\*\***\*\*\*\***\*\*\*\***\_\_\_\_**\*\*\*\***\*\*\*\***\*\*\*\*** ("Healthcare Site" or "Clinic Partner"),

collectively the "Parties."

The purpose of this MOU is to outline how the Parties will work together to host **Medical Assistant learners, externs, or trainees** at the clinic in a way that is safe, compliant, and beneficial to everyone involved.

---

## 1. Purpose

The Parties agree to collaborate to:

- Provide **hands-on Medical Assistant training** and real clinical exposure for approved Learners;
- Develop a **pipeline of Medical Assistants** who are clinic-ready, certification-ready, and patient-ready; and
- Align with **state healthcare regulations, HIPAA, externship requirements, and workforce standards** where applicable.

---

## 2. Scope of Collaboration

Under this MOU, the Healthcare Site may host learners for:

- Medical Assistant externship hours;
- Supervised clinical skills practice (vitals, patient intake, rooming, basic procedures);
- Administrative tasks (scheduling, EHR documentation, phone etiquette, insurance basics);
- Observation and shadowing of providers and clinical teams; and
- Other related training activities approved by Elevate and the Healthcare Site.

The specific schedules, cohorts, and details may be confirmed via email or program documents without reissuing this MOU.

---

## 3. Responsibilities of Elevate for Humanity

Elevate agrees to:

1. **Prepare Learners**
   - Provide or coordinate classroom and lab training following approved Medical Assistant curriculum.
   - Set expectations with Learners around **attendance, professionalism, hygiene, and patient confidentiality**.
   - Ensure Learners complete required health screenings, immunizations, background checks, and HIPAA training as requested by the Healthcare Site.

2. **Support the Clinic**
   - Provide a **point of contact** for clinic leadership to address concerns or questions.
   - Intervene when there are repeated **attendance, conduct, or performance** issues.
   - Assist with basic documentation needed for externship, workforce, or certification pathways where Elevate is the sponsor or partner.

3. **Documentation**
   - Provide or support **hour logs, competency checklists, or evaluation forms** required for externship or certification.
   - Use information from the clinic to update Learner records and support board or workforce reporting.

---

## 4. Responsibilities of the Healthcare Site / Clinic Partner

The Healthcare Site agrees to:

1. **Environment & Professional Standards**
   - Maintain a clinic environment that is **safe, clean, and professional**, in line with **state healthcare regulations, OSHA, and HIPAA**.
   - Clearly communicate clinic rules, expectations, and standards for **appearance, patient interaction, confidentiality, and infection control**.

2. **Supervision & Training**
   - Ensure Learners are supervised by licensed or credentialed clinical staff when performing patient care tasks.
   - Allow Learners to:
     - Practice clinical skills under supervision (vitals, patient intake, rooming, specimen collection, etc.);
     - Observe and assist with basic procedures as permitted by scope and policy;
     - Perform administrative tasks (scheduling, phone calls, EHR documentation) under guidance.
   - Provide basic training in:
     - Clinic workflow and team communication;
     - Patient greeting and customer service;
     - Confidentiality and HIPAA compliance;
     - Infection control and safety protocols.

3. **Attendance, Hours & Feedback**
   - Accurately track **hours, days, and key activities** for each Learner.
   - Notify Elevate of:
     - Repeated tardiness, absences, or no call / no show;
     - Serious concerns about behavior, attitude, patient safety, or confidentiality breaches.
   - Provide **feedback or simple evaluations** when requested to support progress and certification requirements.

4. **Safety, Boundaries & Conduct**
   - Maintain a clinic free from **harassment, discrimination, and abusive behavior**.
   - Avoid assigning Learners work that is:
     - Unsafe, outside their scope of training, or not allowed by state regulations;
     - Far outside the scope of Medical Assistant training (for example, unrelated clerical work as their main activity).
   - Maintain professional boundaries with Learners and inform Elevate of serious incidents (patient safety concerns, HIPAA violations, harassment, threats, substance use impacting safety, etc.).

5. **HIPAA & Confidentiality**
   - Ensure Learners sign clinic-specific HIPAA agreements and confidentiality policies.
   - Provide basic HIPAA orientation and expectations for handling protected health information (PHI).
   - Report any suspected HIPAA violations or confidentiality breaches to Elevate immediately.

---

## 5. Learner Status & Compensation

1. Unless separately and explicitly agreed:
   - Learners/externs in this context are **training participants**, not employees of Elevate.
   - Any **employment relationship** (if created) is between the Healthcare Site and the individual Learner.

2. If the Healthcare Site chooses to pay Learners:
   - The site is responsible for **payroll, taxes, and employment law compliance** related to that decision.

---

## 6. Data, Photos, and Promotion

1. Data & Reporting:
   - The Healthcare Site may share attendance, performance notes, and hour logs with Elevate to support externship, certification, and workforce reporting.
   - Elevate will use these records only for legitimate program, compliance, and quality purposes.

2. Photos & Marketing:
   - The Healthcare Site and Elevate agree to respect:
     - Photo consent from Learners and patients;
     - HIPAA restrictions around patient images and information;
     - Restrictions around public posting for justice-involved individuals when shared by Elevate.
   - Any **co-branded marketing, heavy logo use, or media stories** featuring Elevate should be reviewed with Elevate in advance.

3. Patient Privacy:
   - No patient names, images, or protected health information may be shared in marketing or promotional materials without proper consent and HIPAA compliance.

---

## 7. Health & Safety Requirements

1. **Pre-Placement Requirements:**
   - Learners must complete health screenings, immunizations (MMR, Tdap, Hepatitis B, flu, etc.), TB testing, and background checks as required by the Healthcare Site.
   - Elevate will coordinate these requirements but the Healthcare Site may verify compliance before allowing Learners on-site.

2. **Infection Control:**
   - Learners must follow all clinic infection control policies, including hand hygiene, PPE use, and standard precautions.
   - The Healthcare Site will provide basic orientation on infection control procedures.

3. **Incident Reporting:**
   - Any needlestick injuries, exposure incidents, or safety concerns involving Learners must be reported to both the Healthcare Site and Elevate immediately.

---

## 8. Term, Changes, and Ending the Relationship

1. **Term.**  
   This MOU is effective on the latest signature date below and continues for **one (1) year**, automatically renewing each year unless ended by either Party.

2. **Changes.**  
   Adjustments to details such as number of externs, schedules, or specific support can be made via email or written communication between the Parties.

3. **Termination.**  
   Either Party may end this MOU at any time, with or without cause, by written notice (email is acceptable). The Parties will:
   - Work together to **support current Learners** through a reasonable transition or closure where possible.

---

## 9. Non-Binding & No Guarantee

This MOU:

- Does **not** create a legal partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.
- Does **not** guarantee a specific number of Learners, hours, or hires.
- Does **not** require either Party to pay money to the other, unless separately agreed in writing.
