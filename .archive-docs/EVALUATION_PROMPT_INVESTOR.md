# EVALUATION PROMPT: INVESTOR VERSION

> You are acting as a senior investment analyst specializing in EdTech, workforce development, and early-stage social impact ventures. You evaluate opportunities for impact investors, venture philanthropy funds, and mission-driven capital.

Analyze the following investment opportunity with rigor and realism:

---

## 1. FOUNDER & VENTURE CONTEXT

**Founder Profile:**

- Solo founder, self-taught technical background
- Started February 2025 (10 months execution)
- Built registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit
- No institutional backing, no co-founders, no paid team
- Operating under financial constraints and personal limitations

**Execution to Date:**

- Functional web platform (deployed to production)
- 569 React components, 284 database migrations
- Automated compliance system for government-funded training
- 40+ stakeholder meetings with workforce development organizations
- Follow-ups secured with potential partners

**Investment Ask:**

- Pilot funding: $50K-$100K (6-month runway)
- Seed funding: $250K-$500K (18-month runway)
- Series A: $2M-$5M (scale to 100+ program holders)

---

## 2. PRODUCT & BUSINESS MODEL

**Platform Overview:**

- Workforce training platform combining:
  - AI-assisted LMS
  - Apprenticeship/on-the-job training models
  - Government credential partnerships (ETPL, WIOA, WorkOne)
  - Automated compliance monitoring and reporting
  - Multi-tier licensing model

**Revenue Model:**

- SaaS licensing: $2K-$200K/month per program holder
- Tiered pricing based on size and features
- Recurring revenue (monthly/annual subscriptions)
- Target market: Small nonprofits and community organizations

**Unit Economics (Projected):**

- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): $5,000-$10,000
- Lifetime Value (LTV): $50,000-$500,000
- LTV:CAC Ratio: 10:1 to 50:1
- Gross Margin: 80%+ (SaaS model)
- Payback Period: 6-12 months

**Market Opportunity:**

- Total Addressable Market (TAM): $2B (US workforce training)
- Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM): $200M (Indiana + adjacent states)
- Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM): $20M (small nonprofits in Indiana)

---

## 3. VERIFICATION REQUIREMENT (No Assumptions)

Evaluate only what can be proven through:

- Implemented code paths (GitHub repository)
- Admin interfaces (live platform demo)
- Workflow logic (database schema, migrations)
- Documentation tied to execution (technical docs, compliance research)

**Explicitly separate:**

- **Implemented:** Code exists, deployed, accessible
- **Partially Implemented:** Code exists, not fully tested or deployed
- **Aspirational:** Designed but not built, or built but not operational

**Red Flags to Check:**

- Is this vaporware? (pitch deck only, no code)
- Is this outsourced? (inconsistent code style, template-based)
- Is this overstated? (claims not backed by evidence)
- Is this sustainable? (single founder, no team, no revenue)

---

## 4. MARKET & COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

**Determine:**

1. **Market Validation:**
   - Is there proven demand for this solution?
   - Are customers willing to pay?
   - What's the sales cycle length?
   - What's the churn risk?

2. **Competitive Positioning:**
   - Who are the direct competitors?
   - What's the differentiation?
   - Is the moat defensible?
   - Can incumbents replicate this?

3. **Innovation Assessment:**
   - Is this a novel solution or incremental improvement?
   - Is the innovation technical, operational, or business model?
   - Can this be patented or protected?
   - What's the first-mover advantage?

4. **Market Timing:**
   - Is this the right time for this solution?
   - Are there regulatory tailwinds?
   - Is government funding increasing or decreasing?
   - What are the macro trends?

---

## 5. INVESTMENT ASSESSMENT

**Evaluate:**

### 5.1 Team Risk

- **Single Founder Risk:** How critical is the founder? Can the business survive without them?
- **Technical Depth:** Can the founder scale the product?
- **Operational Capacity:** Can the founder run operations + sales + support?
- **Hiring Plan:** When and who to hire first?

### 5.2 Product Risk

- **Technical Maturity:** Is the platform production-ready?
- **Scalability:** Can it handle 100+ customers?
- **Security:** Is customer data protected?
- **Compliance:** Does it meet regulatory requirements?

### 5.3 Market Risk

- **Demand Validation:** Is there proven customer demand?
- **Sales Cycle:** How long to close a deal?
- **Customer Concentration:** Is revenue diversified?
- **Churn Risk:** Will customers renew?

### 5.4 Financial Risk

- **Burn Rate:** How fast is cash being spent?
- **Runway:** How long until cash runs out?
- **Path to Profitability:** When will revenue exceed costs?
- **Capital Efficiency:** How much capital needed to reach breakeven?

### 5.5 Execution Risk

- **Operational Proof:** Has the model been validated?
- **Pilot Results:** Are there successful case studies?
- **Customer References:** Can customers vouch for the product?
- **Traction Metrics:** What's the growth rate?

---

## 6. INVESTMENT DECISION FRAMEWORK

**Answer directly:**

### 6.1 Fundability

- Is this investable at current stage?
- What stage is this? (Pre-seed, Seed, Series A)
- What's the appropriate check size?
- What milestones must be hit before next round?

### 6.2 Return Potential

- What's the exit potential? (acquisition, IPO, sustainable nonprofit)
- What's the realistic valuation in 5 years?
- What's the expected return multiple? (2x, 5x, 10x)
- What's the risk-adjusted return?

### 6.3 Impact Potential

- What's the social impact? (jobs created, lives improved)
- Is impact measurable?
- Does impact scale with revenue?
- Is this mission-aligned for impact investors?

### 6.4 Investment Structure

- Equity or debt?
- Convertible note or priced round?
- What valuation is fair?
- What terms are appropriate? (board seat, reporting, milestones)

---

## 7. RECOMMENDATION

**Provide:**

1. **Investment Recommendation:** Pass, Maybe, or Invest
2. **Rationale:** Why or why not?
3. **Conditions:** What must change to move from Maybe to Invest?
4. **Valuation Guidance:** What's a fair pre-money valuation?
5. **Deal Structure:** What terms would you propose?
6. **Milestones:** What must be achieved before next round?

---

## 8. PLAIN-LANGUAGE VERDICT

**Answer directly:**

- Is this a good investment opportunity?
- What's the biggest risk?
- What's the biggest opportunity?
- Would you invest your own money?
- What would you tell a friend considering this?

**Do not inflate. Do not diminish.**  
**Be precise, evidence-based, and realistic.**  
**Assume the goal is capital deployment, not hype.**

---

## SUPPORTING DOCUMENTS TO REQUEST

Before making final investment decision, request:

1. **Financial Model:**
   - 3-year revenue projections
   - Unit economics breakdown
   - Burn rate and runway
   - Path to profitability

2. **Pilot Results:**
   - Number of pilot partners
   - Student enrollment data
   - Outcome metrics (employment, credentials)
   - Customer testimonials

3. **Legal Validation:**
   - Legal opinion on credential-sharing model
   - Indiana DWD confirmation
   - MOU review by attorney
   - IP ownership clarity

4. **Technical Audit:**
   - Code review by independent developer
   - Security audit
   - Scalability assessment
   - Technical debt analysis

5. **Market Validation:**
   - Customer interviews (5-10)
   - Competitor analysis
   - Pricing validation
   - Sales pipeline

6. **Team Plan:**
   - Hiring roadmap
   - Org chart (current + 12 months)
   - Advisor/board composition
   - Key person insurance
